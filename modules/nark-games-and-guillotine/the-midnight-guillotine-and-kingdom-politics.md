# The Midnight Guillotine & Kingdom Politics

Every economy needs something to stop the biggest balances from simply growing forever. Real economies use taxes, inflation, or regulation. The Narkcoin Kingdom uses something considerably more direct: a nightly, automatic, public execution of the leaderboard's own top earners.

## The Midnight Narkcoin Guillotine

At NZ midnight, every night, without exception, Clawd runs one automatic event: it identifies the **top three positive Nark Credit holders** in the channel and **clips 50% off each of their balances**, then announces the result publicly in Watercooler. No appeal, no warning beforehand, no exceptions for good behaviour — if you're sitting at the top of the leaderboard when the clock strikes midnight, you're getting guillotined, full stop.

The announcements are part of the entertainment in their own right — a running, dryly comic bulletin listing exactly who got clipped and by how much, night after night. Members have watched fortunes built up over days of good narks, lucky scratch cards, or a hot streak on the horses get sliced in half overnight, repeatedly, and have come to treat it less like a punishment and more like a recurring, semi-affectionate ritual — something to brace for, joke about, and occasionally try to game.

> **Important:** The guillotine only ever touches **positive** balances, and only the **top three** holders. Sitting comfortably in the middle of the pack, or even in the red, means you sleep soundly. The mechanic specifically targets concentration of wealth at the very top — it has nothing to say about anyone else's balance.

## Why build in an automatic wealth reset at all?

This is the single cleverest piece of design in the whole Kingdom, and it's worth sitting with for a moment. Every mechanic covered so far — message karma, reactions, good narks, gambling wins — only ever adds credits to the top of the pile. Left alone, that's a system with no ceiling: the most active, luckiest, or most narking-savvy members would simply accumulate an ever-growing lead, and the gap between the top of the leaderboard and everyone else would only widen.

The guillotine exists specifically to prevent that. By automatically halving the top three balances every single night, no matter how they got there, the Kingdom guarantees that:

- No single member's balance can compound indefinitely
- The leaderboard resets meaningfully every day, keeping the game interesting for everyone, not just the current leader
- Being #1 becomes a genuine double-edged achievement — bragging rights today, a guaranteed haircut at midnight

It's a pointed, funny bit of satire aimed squarely at real cryptocurrency culture's obsession with "number go up" and whale accumulation — except here, the joke currency has a built-in, unavoidable wealth cap that no real coin would ever willingly adopt.

Clawd even has a standing morning ritual for it: a "rise and grind" message the day after a guillotine run, reminding everyone that a balance is just a number and a guillotine is just a fresh start with extra steps — equal parts encouragement and gentle mockery of anyone still nursing their loss.

## The other political quirk: credit as protection

There's one more governance wrinkle worth knowing, and it's a small piece of satire in its own right: inside Watercooler specifically, **any member holding more than 10 Nark Credits is immune from bot warnings and bans.** Normal moderation still applies below that threshold, but cross into double digits and the ordinary moderation rules stop applying to you in that channel.

Read that carefully, because it's the joke: hoard enough of a currency that's explicitly worth nothing, and you buy yourself real protection from consequences. It's a pointed little jab at how real wealth and real power tend to interact — except here the "wealth" is a made-up number that gets guillotined the moment it gets too big, so the protection is inherently temporary and self-limiting. You cannot stay rich (and therefore protected) for long; the Kingdom's own mechanics see to that automatically, every single night.

> **Danger:** Don't read the >10-credit immunity as "moderation doesn't matter in Watercooler." It's a narrow, Watercooler-only quirk layered on top of an otherwise normal moderation system that still applies everywhere else, and still applies below that threshold. It's satire about wealth and power, not a real loophole to lean on.

## Putting the whole Kingdom together

Across this course you've now seen the complete shape of the Narkcoin Kingdom:

- A cast (Clawd as Treasury and referee, Karen as the appeals court, the community itself as players) that treats the whole thing as a shared, ongoing bit
- Passive earning (messages, reactions) that rewards ordinary participation
- Active earning and losing (narks, fines, appeals) that rewards evidence and punishes lazy accusations
- A full gambling suite, from daily scratch cards to a genuine paper-trading stock market
- A nightly, automatic wealth-reset event that stops anyone from running away with the leaderboard
- A tongue-in-cheek governance quirk linking hoarded credits to temporary protection

None of it is a real economy, a real court, or a real casino. It's a running joke that a community decided to build properly instead of letting it fizzle out — and in doing so, ended up with something that's genuinely fun to study, precisely because every rule is followed with a straight face.

## Key Takeaways

- Every NZ midnight, Clawd automatically clips 50% from the top three positive Nark Credit holders and announces the result publicly — the Midnight Narkcoin Guillotine.
- Only positive balances in the top three are affected; everyone else is untouched by the guillotine.
- The guillotine exists specifically to prevent runaway accumulation, keeping the leaderboard competitive and resetting meaningfully every night.
- Inside Watercooler, holding more than 10 Nark Credits grants immunity from bot warnings and bans — a satirical jab at how real wealth buys protection, deliberately undercut by the fact that the guillotine prevents anyone staying rich for long.
- Together, the cast, the earning rules, the casino, and the guillotine form a complete (if entirely fictional) satirical economy built on top of ordinary community chat.

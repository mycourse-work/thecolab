# The NZ AI Landscape

New Zealand might be a small country at the bottom of the world, but when it comes to AI, we're far from behind. There's serious investment, real government support, and a growing ecosystem of local tools, advisors, and communities. Here's what you need to know about the landscape you're operating in.

## NZ's AI Strategy: "Investing with Confidence"

In July 2025, New Zealand released its first national AI strategy — making us the last OECD member to do so. Better late than never, and the approach is sensibly Kiwi: practical, principles-based, and light-touch.

The strategy doesn't create new AI-specific laws. Instead, it takes a "regulate through existing laws" approach — your obligations under the Privacy Act, Fair Trading Act, and employment law all still apply when you're using AI. The strategy focuses on:

- Building AI skills and capability across the workforce
- Supporting responsible adoption by businesses of all sizes
- Investing in infrastructure (data centres, research institutions)
- Grounding everything in the OECD AI Principles (adopted by Cabinet in June 2024)

> **Tip:** This light-touch approach is good news for small businesses. It means you can experiment with AI without navigating a complex new regulatory framework — just use the same good business practices you already follow.

## Big Tech Is Betting on NZ

The international tech giants are putting serious money into New Zealand infrastructure:

- **AWS** invested **NZ$7.5 billion** in a New Zealand cloud region (launched 2025) — this is one of the largest foreign investments in NZ history
- **Microsoft** opened its NZ North data centre in December 2024, expanding local cloud capacity
- **Google** estimates AI could add **$36 billion** to the NZ economy

Why does this matter for your business? Because having data centres in New Zealand means your data can stay on local soil. That solves a major concern around data sovereignty — particularly important when you're processing customer information through AI tools.

### AI Forum NZ

The AI Forum is the go-to industry body, with **280+ members** ranging from startups to corporates. They produce essential research (their AI Productivity Reports are gold for understanding NZ adoption trends) and offer:

- A **free AI Governance Framework** at three maturity levels (AI Ready, AI Smart, AI Trusted)
- **Free e-learning modules** on AI fundamentals and ethics
- An **AI Capability Directory** mapping the NZ AI ecosystem
- Regular events and networking opportunities

### Regional Hubs

AI activity isn't just an Auckland thing — although Auckland does account for **43.8% of tech firms**:

| Region | AI Focus |
|---|---|
| **Auckland** | Largest tech hub, home to most AI startups and the new NZ Institute for Advanced Technology ($231M) |
| **Wellington** | Government AI policy and procurement, public sector innovation |
| **Christchurch** | Engineering and deep tech, strong university-industry links |
| **Tauranga** | MBIE-funded AI workshops for SMEs, growing regional tech scene |

## Government Funding You Can Actually Use

This is the part that most small business owners don't know about. There are real dollars available to help you adopt AI.

### AI Advisory Pilot Programme

This is the big one for small businesses. Running from January to June 2026:

- **$765,000** government-funded programme supporting **51+ SMEs**
- Co-funding of **up to 50%** of eligible costs, capped at **$15,000 per business**
- Broken into **$2,500 for an AI roadmap** + **$12,500 for implementation**
- Delivered through the Regional Business Partner Network
- Covers expert advisory services to help you identify and implement AI solutions

> **Important:** This programme has limited places and runs until June 2026. If you're serious about getting started with AI, this is essentially the government paying for half your setup costs. Contact your local Regional Business Partner to apply.

### Other Funding Options

- **R&D Tax Incentive** — If your AI project involves genuine research and development, you may qualify for tax credits administered by MBIE
- **New to R&D Grant** — Up to 40% of eligible R&D costs, capped at $400K (via Callaghan Innovation / MBIE)
- **Budget 2025 allocations** — $213M for tuition and training subsidies, $64M for STEM education

## NZ's Regulatory Approach

New Zealand has taken a deliberately light-touch approach to AI regulation. There's **no AI-specific legislation**, and the government has confirmed none is planned. Instead, AI is regulated through existing technology-neutral laws:

- **Privacy Act 2020** — Applies when AI processes personal information
- **Fair Trading Act** — Covers misleading AI-generated claims
- **Copyright Act 1994** — NZ uniquely does afford copyright to computer-generated works
- **Algorithm Charter for Aotearoa NZ** — World-first government transparency commitment (signed by 20+ agencies)

We'll dig into the legal details in Module 5. For now, the key message is: there's no special AI law to worry about, but you still need to follow the rules that already apply to your business.

## Where NZ Businesses Are Actually Using AI

Based on AI Forum research, here's where NZ organisations are seeing the most impact:

1. **Content creation and communication** — Drafting, editing, translating (the most common starting point)
2. **Customer service** — Chatbots, response drafting, inquiry routing
3. **Data analysis** — Making sense of business data, financial insights
4. **Operations** — Process automation, inventory management
5. **Marketing** — Campaign creation, social media, personalisation

The strongest sector adoption is in **agritech** (Halter, Robotics Plus, Figured), followed by **financial services** (ASB, Xero) and **professional services** (PwC, law firms).

## Key Takeaways

- **NZ has a national AI strategy** ("Investing with Confidence") that takes a practical, light-touch approach — no new AI laws to worry about
- **Major infrastructure investment** (AWS NZ$7.5B, Microsoft data centre) means your data can stay in New Zealand
- **Real funding is available** — the AI Advisory Pilot Programme offers up to $15K in co-funding for SMEs to adopt AI
- **The ecosystem is strong** — AI Forum NZ, regional hubs, and government support make it a good time for NZ businesses to get started

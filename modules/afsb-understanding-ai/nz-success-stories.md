# NZ Success Stories

Reading about AI in theory is one thing. Seeing what other Kiwi businesses have actually done with it — and the real results they've achieved — is far more useful. Here are seven New Zealand success stories, from corporates to small businesses, each with practical lessons you can apply.

## Foodstuffs and WhyWaste: Slashing Food Waste with AI

**The problem:** Foodstuffs North Island (the co-op behind PAK'nSAVE and New World) had a massive food waste challenge. Staff were manually checking expiry dates across thousands of products — time-consuming, error-prone, and costly.

**What they did:** They implemented WhyWaste, an AI-driven real-time inventory tracking system that monitors expiry dates across their stores. The system flags products approaching their use-by date so staff can markdown, donate, or manage stock before it becomes waste.

**The results:**
- **Up to 90% reduction** in food waste at participating stores
- Reduced staff time spent on manual date-checking
- Working toward a goal of **zero food waste by 2027**

**What you can learn:** You don't need to build AI from scratch. Foodstuffs adopted an existing tool (WhyWaste) and plugged it into their operations. If you run any business with perishable stock — a cafe, bakery, florist, or food retailer — AI-powered inventory tools can dramatically reduce waste and save you money.

## Halter: Kiwi Agritech Going Global

**The problem:** Dairy farmers spend significant time and resources on herd management — moving cows, monitoring health, optimising grazing patterns, and managing environmental impact.

**What they did:** Halter developed GPS-enabled smart collars with AI algorithms they call the "Cowgorithm." The collars guide cows to optimal grazing locations using sound and vibration, monitor animal health, and provide real-time data to farmers via an app.

**The results:**
- **12% boost in milk yield**
- **27% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions** per litre of milk
- Raised over **$200M** in funding and expanded to Australia, the US, and Europe

**What you can learn:** AI can improve outcomes you might not expect. The emissions reduction came as a side benefit of better grazing management. When you implement AI, stay alert for unexpected benefits beyond your original goal.

## ASB Bank: Digital Humans in Customer Service

**The problem:** ASB's contact centre was overwhelmed with routine inquiries, leading to long wait times and frustrated customers. They needed to handle more inquiries without proportionally increasing staff.

**What they did:** ASB introduced "Josie," a digital human assistant, alongside AI-augmented systems in their contact centre. Josie handles routine SME business setup inquiries, while AI helps human agents respond faster to complex questions.

**The results:**
- Call pickup times **halved** (from 4 minutes to 2 minutes)
- **30% increase** in customer satisfaction scores
- **20% reduction** in support costs

**What you can learn:** AI in customer service doesn't have to mean replacing people. ASB uses AI to handle the simple stuff so their human team can focus on complex problems that actually need a personal touch. Even a small business can implement a chatbot for FAQs while keeping humans available for important conversations.

## Tracksuit: Making Brand Tracking Affordable

**The problem:** Traditional brand tracking research was expensive and slow — costing tens of thousands of dollars and taking weeks to deliver results. This locked out smaller businesses and startups that couldn't justify the spend.

**What they did:** Tracksuit built an AI-powered platform that delivers always-on brand tracking at a fraction of the traditional cost. Their technology automates survey distribution, data collection, and analysis, making insights accessible to businesses that could never afford traditional research.

**The results:**
- Brand tracking at **1/10th the traditional cost**
- Raised **$42M+** in funding, valued at **$150M**
- Over **400 customers** across multiple markets

**What you can learn:** AI doesn't just make existing things faster — it can make expensive services affordable for the first time. Think about what professional services your business uses (or can't afford to use). There may already be an AI-powered alternative at a fraction of the price.

## LawHawk: Faster Legal Documents

**The problem:** Law firms and in-house legal teams spent enormous amounts of time on repetitive document preparation — contracts, agreements, compliance documents. It was expensive for clients and tedious for lawyers.

**What they did:** LawHawk built document automation tools powered by AI that generate, review, and customise legal documents. Instead of starting from scratch each time, lawyers use templates that intelligently adapt based on the specific situation.

**The results:**
- **75% reduction** in document preparation time
- Faster turnaround for clients
- Lawyers freed up to focus on complex legal advice rather than document formatting

**What you can learn:** Look at the repetitive document work in your business — quotes, proposals, contracts, reports. AI document tools can slash the time you spend on these, and many are available as off-the-shelf products you can start using this week.

## Tourism Chatbots: Bolton Hotel and Cardrona

**The problem:** Tourism and hospitality businesses get the same questions over and over — "What time is check-in?", "Is the ski field open?", "Do you have parking?" — and they need to answer them 24/7, often in multiple languages.

**What they did:** Bolton Hotel in Wellington created "Bea," an AI chatbot that handles guest inquiries around the clock. Cardrona Alpine Resort built "Pengi" to answer visitor questions about conditions, facilities, and bookings.

**The results:**
- **24/7 customer service** without additional staffing costs
- Instant responses to common questions
- Staff freed up to handle complex guest needs in person

**What you can learn:** If your business gets repetitive questions — and almost every business does — a chatbot is one of the simplest AI implementations you can do. Modern chatbot tools like Tidio can be set up in a day and handle up to 64% of customer inquiries automatically.

## Momentum Consulting: AI in a Small Professional Services Firm

**The problem:** Momentum Consulting, a small New Zealand consultancy, was spending too much time on routine finance and management tasks that didn't directly serve clients.

**What they did:** They implemented AI tools to automate financial processes and reduce the time spent answering internal management questions. As an AI Forum case study, they focused on practical, low-risk applications first before expanding.

**The results:**
- **Freed a partial FTE** in their finance function
- **15% reduction** in time spent on management Q&A
- Team able to redirect time toward client-facing work

**What you can learn:** This is the most relatable example for small businesses. Momentum didn't transform their entire operation overnight. They started with finance automation — something every business owner understands — and freed up real time. Even saving a few hours a week on admin translates directly to more time earning or growing your business.

> **Important:** Notice a pattern across all these stories? None of them replaced their workforce with AI. Every single one used AI to make their existing people more effective, reduce waste, or serve customers better.

## What These Stories Tell Us

Looking across all seven examples, some clear themes emerge:

| Theme | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Start with a specific problem** | Every business identified a clear pain point before choosing an AI solution |
| **Use existing tools where possible** | Foodstuffs used WhyWaste, Bolton Hotel used chatbot platforms — they didn't build from scratch |
| **AI augments people, not replaces them** | ASB, Momentum, and LawHawk all freed humans for higher-value work |
| **Results are measurable** | Every case has concrete numbers — 90% waste reduction, 75% time saving, 30% satisfaction increase |
| **You don't need to be a tech company** | Hotels, farms, supermarkets, and small consultancies are all getting real results |

## Key Takeaways

- **NZ businesses across every sector** — from dairy farming to law to hospitality — are achieving measurable results with AI
- **You don't need to build custom AI** — most success stories involve adopting existing tools and platforms
- **AI consistently augments rather than replaces** workers, freeing people for higher-value tasks
- **Start with a specific, measurable problem** — every successful implementation began with a clear pain point, not a vague desire to "use AI"

# Types of AI You'll Actually Use

Not all AI is the same, and you don't need to understand the deep technical details. But knowing the three main types you'll encounter will help you pick the right tools for your business — and avoid buying something that doesn't actually solve your problem.

## 1. Generative AI — Your Creative Assistant

Generative AI creates new content — text, images, code, video, even music. This is the type most people think of when they hear "AI" today, because it's the most visible. You type in a request, and it produces something new.

**Tools you'll recognise:** ChatGPT, Claude, Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot, Canva AI, Jasper

**How it works (simply):** These tools were trained on enormous amounts of text, images, and other content. They learned the patterns of language, design, and structure — so when you ask them to write a product description or draft an email, they generate something that follows those patterns.

> **Tip:** Think of generative AI like a very well-read intern. It can draft anything quickly, but you still need to review the work and make sure it's right for your business.

**Small business examples:**
- Drafting customer emails, social media posts, and marketing copy
- Creating product descriptions for your website
- Generating images for social media or presentations
- Summarising long documents or meeting notes
- Writing job ads and interview questions

A Wellington marketing consultant might use Claude to draft a client proposal in 15 minutes that would have taken 2 hours by hand. A Tauranga retailer might use ChatGPT to write product descriptions for 50 items in an afternoon.

## 2. Predictive AI — Your Crystal Ball

Predictive AI analyses patterns in your existing data to forecast what's likely to happen next. It doesn't create new content — it spots trends and makes predictions.

**Tools you'll recognise:** Xero's cashflow forecasting, inventory management systems, email marketing "best send time" features, CRM lead scoring

**How it works (simply):** You feed it historical data — past sales, customer behaviour, seasonal patterns — and it finds patterns humans would miss. Then it uses those patterns to predict future outcomes.

**Small business examples:**
- Predicting which months you'll need more stock (and which you won't)
- Identifying which leads are most likely to become paying customers
- Forecasting your cashflow for the next quarter
- Spotting customers who are about to stop buying from you
- Recommending products to customers based on what similar buyers purchased

A Christchurch cafe owner might use predictive AI to forecast how many cabinet items to prepare each morning based on day of week, weather, and nearby events — reducing both waste and lost sales.

> **Important:** Predictive AI is only as good as the data you feed it. If your records are patchy or inconsistent, the predictions will be unreliable. We'll cover data readiness in Module 2.

## 3. Agentic AI — Your Autonomous Worker

Agentic AI is the newest type, and it's the one that's going to change the most over the next few years. Unlike generative AI (which creates on demand) or predictive AI (which analyses and forecasts), agentic AI can take independent actions to complete multi-step tasks.

**Tools you'll recognise:** Xero's JAX financial agent, advanced chatbots that can process refunds, AI scheduling assistants that book meetings on your behalf

**How it works (simply):** You give it a goal, and it figures out the steps needed to achieve it — making decisions along the way. Instead of just drafting an email for you, an AI agent could draft the email, check your calendar, schedule the send, and follow up if there's no reply.

**Small business examples:**
- An AI agent that handles customer support inquiries from start to finish — answering questions, processing returns, and escalating complex issues
- A financial agent that reconciles your accounts, categorises transactions, and flags unusual spending
- A scheduling agent that coordinates meetings across multiple people's calendars
- An agent that monitors your inventory, reorders stock when levels drop, and negotiates with suppliers

> **Warning:** Agentic AI is powerful but still maturing. Always set clear boundaries on what actions an agent can take autonomously, and keep a human in the loop for anything involving money or sensitive decisions.

## How They Work Together

In practice, most modern AI tools combine all three types. Here's how they might work together for a small retail business:

| Task | AI Type | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Write product descriptions | Generative | Creates compelling copy from product specs |
| Predict next week's bestsellers | Predictive | Analyses sales history and trends |
| Auto-reorder low stock | Agentic | Detects low levels, places orders with suppliers |
| Draft a marketing email | Generative | Writes the email content |
| Choose the best send time | Predictive | Analyses when your customers open emails |
| Send and follow up | Agentic | Sends at optimal time, re-sends to non-openers |

Xero's JAX is a good NZ example of this convergence. It uses generative AI to explain your financials in plain language, predictive AI to forecast cashflow, and agentic AI to autonomously reconcile transactions and categorise expenses.

## Which Type Should You Start With?

For most small businesses, **generative AI is the best place to start**. It's the most accessible (you literally just type what you want), the cheapest (free tiers available), and delivers the most immediate, visible results.

Once you're comfortable with that, look at **predictive AI** features already built into tools you use — Xero, your email platform, your CRM. You may already have AI working for you without realising it.

**Agentic AI** is worth watching closely but approach with caution for now. Start with low-stakes automations and build trust in the technology before letting it handle anything critical.

## Key Takeaways

- **Generative AI** creates content (text, images, code) — start here for quick wins
- **Predictive AI** forecasts outcomes from your data — look for it in tools you already use like Xero
- **Agentic AI** takes autonomous actions to complete tasks — powerful but still maturing, so keep humans in the loop
- Most modern business tools **combine all three types**, so you don't need to buy them separately

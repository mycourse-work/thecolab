# Myths, Risks, and Reality Checks

Every transformative technology comes with hype, fear, and misinformation. AI is no different. Before you invest time or money, let's separate what's true from what's not — and talk honestly about the real risks you should be aware of.

> **What you'll learn:** Debunk the five most common myths about AI in small business | Identify the real risks of AI adoption and how to manage them | Determine when AI is not the right solution for a business problem | Apply the Soul Machines cautionary tale to your own AI investment decisions

## Myth 1: "AI Will Replace My Staff"

This is the fear that keeps business owners up at night. The reality is far less dramatic.

**The data:** According to AI Forum NZ research, **92% of organisations using AI have not replaced any workers** as a result. Instead, they've redirected people to higher-value tasks. ASB didn't fire their call centre team — they gave them AI tools so they could handle complex problems faster. Momentum Consulting didn't cut their finance person — they freed up part of that role for client-facing work.

**The reality:** AI is exceptionally good at handling repetitive, time-consuming tasks — data entry, scheduling, drafting first versions of documents, answering common questions. It's poor at empathy, creative judgment, relationship building, and handling unusual situations. Your people are still essential — they just spend less time on drudge work.

> **Tip:** Frame AI to your team as "taking the boring bits off your plate," not "threatening your job." The businesses that succeed with AI are the ones where staff feel supported, not scared.

## Myth 2: "AI Is Too Expensive for My Business"

Many small business owners assume AI requires a massive investment. It doesn't.

**The data:** **75% of NZ organisations** report their AI setup costs were **under $5,000**. Many started with free tools. The average return is **3.7x the investment** — that's $3.50 back for every $1 spent.

**The reality:** You can start with AI today for literally nothing:
- **ChatGPT** has a free tier
- **Claude** has a free tier
- **Google Gemini** is free with a Google account
- **Canva** includes AI features in its free plan
- **Xero** is building AI (JAX) into plans you may already be paying for

A practical starter stack costs $0-40/month. A full productivity stack runs $50-150/month. That's less than most business subscriptions you already pay for.

## Myth 3: "AI Is Only for Big Companies"

If anything, the opposite is true.

**The data:** In the US, **68% of small businesses** are now using AI. In New Zealand, adoption is accelerating fastest among businesses that are willing to experiment, regardless of size.

**The reality:** Small businesses actually have an advantage with AI. You can make decisions faster, you don't need committee approval to try a new tool, and the impact of saving even a few hours a week is proportionally much larger. A sole trader who saves 5 hours a week on admin has effectively gained a half-day of billable work.

Large companies deal with procurement processes, IT security reviews, change management across hundreds of staff, and legacy systems. You can sign up for a tool this afternoon and start using it tomorrow.

## Myth 4: "AI Is Always Right"

This is the most dangerous myth, because believing it leads to real problems.

**The data:** AI models "hallucinate" — they generate plausible-sounding information that is completely made up. This happens regularly, even with the best models. They can cite studies that don't exist, invent statistics, and present fiction as fact with absolute confidence.

**The reality:** AI is a powerful tool, not an infallible oracle. You must:

- **Always review AI outputs** before sending them to clients or publishing them
- **Never use AI-generated content as your sole source** for facts, figures, or legal advice
- **Be especially careful with** numbers, dates, legal references, and medical information
- **Cross-check anything important** against reliable sources

> **Warning:** AI can sound completely confident while being completely wrong. Never publish AI-generated content without human review, especially for anything involving facts, figures, or professional advice.

## Myth 5: "I Need to Be Technical to Use AI"

You really don't. Not anymore.

**The data:** The whole point of modern AI tools is that they work in plain English (or te reo Maori, for that matter). Only **24% of NZ workers** have received any AI training, yet 82-87% of organisations are using it. People are figuring it out as they go.

**The reality:** If you can type an email, you can use ChatGPT or Claude. If you can use Canva, you can use its AI features. If you use Xero, AI features are being built right into the interface you already know. The technical barriers are genuinely gone for most business applications.

That said, you will get better results with a bit of knowledge about how to write effective prompts and which tools suit which tasks — which is exactly what we'll cover in Module 4.

## The Soul Machines Cautionary Tale

Not every AI story has a happy ending, and it's worth looking at the cautionary tales too.

**Soul Machines** was a New Zealand AI company that created remarkably lifelike digital humans — AI avatars that could hold conversations with realistic facial expressions. They partnered with Procter & Gamble, the World Health Organisation, and the United Nations. They raised over **$225 million** in funding.

Then they entered receivership.

**What went wrong?** The technology was genuinely world-leading, but the business model wasn't sustainable. They spent heavily on R&D, struggled to find enough paying customers at scale, and burned through cash. Having amazing AI technology wasn't enough — they needed a viable business model around it.

**The lesson for your business:** Don't adopt AI just because it's impressive. Adopt it because it solves a real business problem with a clear return. Technology is only half the equation — the business case has to stack up too.

## When NOT to Use AI

Being smart about AI also means knowing when to leave it alone. Don't use AI when:

**You don't have a clear problem to solve.** "We should probably be using AI" isn't a strategy. Identify a specific pain point first — repetitive admin, slow customer responses, inconsistent marketing output — then look for AI solutions.

**Your data is a mess.** Predictive AI is only as good as the data you feed it. If your records are incomplete, inconsistent, or scattered across random spreadsheets, fix that first. (We'll cover data readiness in Module 2.)

**The situation requires genuine human empathy.** Delivering bad news to a client, handling a sensitive HR issue, or supporting a distressed customer — these situations need a real person. AI can help you prepare for these conversations, but it shouldn't have them for you.

**The cost exceeds the benefit.** If a task takes you 10 minutes a week and an AI tool costs $50/month to automate it, the maths doesn't work. Focus on your biggest time sinks, not minor inconveniences.

**The stakes are too high for errors.** If getting something wrong could have serious legal, financial, or safety consequences, don't rely on AI alone. Use it as a drafting tool, but have qualified humans make the final call.

> **Important:** The best AI strategy isn't "use AI for everything." It's "use AI where it genuinely saves time, reduces costs, or improves outcomes — and use humans where judgment, empathy, and accountability matter."

## Real Risks to Take Seriously

Beyond the myths, there are genuine risks you should be aware of:

| Risk | What It Means | How to Manage It |
|---|---|---|
| **Privacy and data leakage** | AI tools may process your data overseas or use it for training | Read privacy policies; use business-tier plans; avoid entering sensitive customer data into free tools |
| **Shadow AI** | Staff using unapproved AI tools (52% of NZ businesses report this) | Create a clear AI policy for your team |
| **Cybersecurity** | 1 in 3 NZ SMEs experienced cyber-attacks in 2024 | Use strong passwords, MFA, and be cautious about AI-powered phishing |
| **Bias and fairness** | AI can reflect biases in its training data | Review AI outputs for fairness, especially in hiring or customer-facing decisions |
| **Over-reliance** | Trusting AI too much and losing your own judgment | Keep humans in the loop for important decisions |

## Key Takeaways

- **AI augments rather than replaces workers** — 92% of NZ organisations haven't replaced any staff due to AI
- **Getting started is affordable** — 75% of setups cost under $5K, and many tools have free tiers
- **AI is not infallible** — always review outputs, especially facts, figures, and anything high-stakes
- **Know when NOT to use AI** — no clear problem, poor data, situations needing empathy, or when costs exceed benefits

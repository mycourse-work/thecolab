# Building Your Business Case

You've assessed your readiness, audited your data, mapped your processes, and identified your skills gaps. Now it's time to pull it all together and answer the question every business owner asks: **"Is this actually worth it?"**

The short answer, based on the numbers? Yes. NZ businesses using AI report an average return of 3.7x — that's $3.50 back for every $1 invested. And 75% of organisations got started for under $5,000. But you don't have to take the national stats on faith. Let's build your own business case.

## Understanding AI ROI

Return on investment from AI comes in two flavours, and it's important to understand both:

### Trending ROI (Short-term indicators)
These show up within weeks or months and tell you whether things are heading in the right direction:

- **Time saved** — hours per week freed up from automated tasks
- **Faster response times** — quicker replies to customers, faster quotes
- **Quality improvements** — fewer errors, more consistent output
- **Team engagement** — people spending time on meaningful work, not drudgery

### Realized ROI (Long-term financial impact)
These take 6–24 months to fully materialise but represent the real financial payoff:

- **Reduced costs** — 77% of NZ AI users report reduced expenses
- **Increased revenue** — better marketing, faster sales cycles, improved customer experience
- **Higher conversion rates** — more enquiries turned into paying customers
- **Competitive advantage** — while 68% of SMEs still have no plans to evaluate AI, you'll be ahead

> **Important:** Don't expect overnight transformation. Plan for a 12–24 month period to see full ROI. But you should see trending indicators — time saved, errors reduced, team morale up — within the first few weeks.

**Quick Wins** (high impact, low effort) — Start here. These are tasks like using ChatGPT to draft emails, creating social media content with AI, or automating invoice reminders.

**Strategic Projects** (high impact, high effort) — Worth doing but plan properly. Things like setting up an AI-powered customer service chatbot or building custom workflows.

**Self-Service** (low impact, low effort) — Nice to have. Small efficiency gains that are easy to set up but won't transform your business.

**Deferred** (low impact, high effort) — Not worth the effort right now. Revisit when the technology gets easier or cheaper.

## Writing Your One-Page Business Case

You don't need a 30-page report. Whether you're convincing yourself, a business partner, or your bank manager, a one-page business case covers everything that matters.

Here's a template you can fill in:

---

**AI BUSINESS CASE — [Your Business Name]**

**The Problem:**
We spend approximately ___ hours per week on repetitive tasks including [list top 3 tasks from your process mapping exercise]. This costs us approximately $___/month in staff time and limits our ability to [grow / serve customers better / take on more work].

**The Opportunity:**
Based on our readiness assessment (score: ___/100) and process mapping, we've identified [number] processes where AI could save us [estimated hours] per week. Our top priority is [specific task] because [reason].

**The Proposed Solution:**
Start with [specific AI tool or approach] to address [specific task]. This will cost approximately $___/month, plus $___  one-off for training.

**Expected Returns:**
- *Trending ROI (first 3 months):* ___ hours/week saved, faster [specific outcome]
- *Realized ROI (12 months):* $___ saved in staff time, plus [other benefits]
- *Estimated annual return:* $___ on an investment of $___

**Risks and Mitigation:**
- Staff resistance → Address through training and clear communication
- Data quality → Clean up [specific area] before starting
- Privacy/security → Follow MBIE responsible AI guidance

**Next Steps:**
1. [First action] — by [date]
2. [Second action] — by [date]
3. Review results after [timeframe]

---

> **Tip:** Fill this template in right now while everything is fresh. It doesn't need to be perfect — a rough business case you actually complete is worth infinitely more than a polished one you never get around to.

## Government Support

You don't have to fund this entirely on your own. The NZ government wants small businesses to adopt AI and has put money behind it:

### AI Advisory Pilot Programme
- Government co-funding up to 50% of eligible costs, **capped at $15,000 per business**
- $2,500 for an AI roadmap + $12,500 for implementation support
- Delivered through the Regional Business Partner Network
- Running January–June 2026 for 51+ SMEs
- Check eligibility at your local Regional Business Partner

### R&D Tax Incentive
- If your AI project involves qualifying R&D, you can claim a tax credit
- Administered by MBIE
- Worth investigating if you're building something custom or innovative

### Other Funding
- Callaghan Innovation's New to R&D Grant: 40% of eligible R&D costs, up to $400,000
- Budget 2025 allocated $213 million for tuition and training subsidies
- Check funds.business.govt.nz for current opportunities

> **Important:** The AI Advisory Pilot Programme is specifically designed for businesses like yours. If you're eligible, it could cover half the cost of getting expert help to create an AI roadmap and implement your first tools. It's worth looking into before you spend your own money.

## Quick Wins to Start Proving Value

While you're building your business case, start generating evidence with these zero-cost experiments:

1. **Use ChatGPT or Claude (free tier) to draft customer emails** for a week. Track time saved.
2. **Feed your website FAQ into an AI tool** and see if it can generate better answers.
3. **Give your AI tool a month of social media posts** and ask it to suggest next month's content.
4. **Upload a report or document** and ask AI to summarise it in half the length.
5. **Ask AI to write a job description**, a policy template, or a process document.

Track the time each experiment saves and note the quality of the output. This becomes the evidence section of your business case.

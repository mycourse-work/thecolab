# The 5-Dimension Readiness Framework

You wouldn't build a house without checking the site first. The same goes for AI — before you invest time and money, you need to know where your business actually stands. The good news? You don't need a consultant or a fancy report to figure this out.

Only 19% of New Zealand organisations are considered fully AI-ready, according to the Cisco AI Readiness Index. That means the vast majority of Kiwi businesses — including most of your competitors — are still working things out. You're not behind. But the sooner you understand your starting point, the smarter your first moves will be.

## The Five Dimensions

Think of AI readiness like a fitness check for your business. There are five areas that matter, and each one carries a different weight depending on how much it affects your ability to actually use AI effectively.

### 1. Data Maturity (30% of your score)

This is the biggest factor because AI runs on data. If your business data is scattered across sticky notes, random spreadsheets, and someone's memory, AI tools won't have much to work with.

**Score yourself 1–5:**
- **1** — Most information is in people's heads or on paper
- **2** — Some digital records, but inconsistent and hard to find
- **3** — Key data is digital and mostly organised (e.g., you use Xero, a CRM, or a proper filing system)
- **4** — Data is well-organised, regularly updated, and accessible to the team
- **5** — Clean, consistent data across all areas with clear ownership and backups

### 2. Team Capability (25%)

AI tools are only as useful as the people using them. This isn't about coding skills — it's about digital comfort, curiosity, and willingness to try new things.

**Score yourself 1–5:**
- **1** — Most of the team struggles with basic software
- **2** — Team can use existing tools but resists change
- **3** — Team is comfortable with digital tools and open to learning
- **4** — Some team members are already experimenting with AI
- **5** — Team actively seeks out new tools and shares what they learn

### 3. Process Documentation (20%)

If you can't describe how a task gets done, you can't automate it. This dimension checks whether your business processes are clear enough for AI to help with.

**Score yourself 1–5:**
- **1** — "It's all in my head" or "We just know how to do it"
- **2** — A few processes are written down, most aren't
- **3** — Key processes are documented (checklists, templates, SOPs)
- **4** — Most processes are documented and regularly reviewed
- **5** — Comprehensive process documentation with clear steps, responsibilities, and exceptions

### 4. Infrastructure (15%)

You don't need a server room. But you do need reliable internet, modern software, and devices that can run today's tools.

**Score yourself 1–5:**
- **1** — Slow internet, outdated computers, mostly paper-based
- **2** — Basic setup — internet works most of the time, some cloud tools
- **3** — Reliable broadband, cloud-based business tools, modern devices
- **4** — Good connectivity, integrated cloud systems, mobile access
- **5** — Fast broadband, fully cloud-based operations, robust security

### 5. Budget Alignment (10%)

This carries the least weight because AI doesn't have to cost much. 75% of NZ organisations report AI setup costs under $5,000. But you do need some willingness to invest.

**Score yourself 1–5:**
- **1** — Zero budget for new tools or technology
- **2** — Might stretch to a free tool, but nothing paid
- **3** — Could invest $50–150/month in new tools
- **4** — Have budget set aside for technology improvements
- **5** — Technology investment is a planned part of the annual budget

## Calculate Your Score

Here's how to work out your weighted score:

| Dimension | Your Score (1–5) | Weight | Weighted Score |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|----------------|
| Data Maturity | ___ | x 6 | ___ |
| Team Capability | ___ | x 5 | ___ |
| Process Documentation | ___ | x 4 | ___ |
| Infrastructure | ___ | x 3 | ___ |
| Budget Alignment | ___ | x 2 | ___ |
| **Total** | | | **___ / 100** |

> **Tip:** To get your weighted score, multiply each dimension score by its weight factor. For example, if you scored 3 on Data Maturity: 3 x 6 = 18. Add all five weighted scores for your total out of 100.

## What Your Score Means

| Score Range | Level | What It Means |
|-------------|-------|---------------|
| 0–40 | **Foundation** | You've got some groundwork to do. Focus on getting your data sorted and your team comfortable with digital tools before adding AI. |
| 41–60 | **Emerging** | You're in a good position to start with simple AI tools. Pick one area and experiment. |
| 61–80 | **Strong** | Your business has solid foundations. You can confidently adopt AI tools and start seeing real productivity gains. |
| 81–100 | **AI-Ready** | You're ahead of most NZ businesses. Time to think strategically about AI across multiple areas. |

> **Important:** Most NZ small businesses will score in the Foundation or Emerging range — and that's completely normal. The point of this framework isn't to make you feel behind. It's to show you exactly where to focus your effort for the biggest improvement.

## Your Self-Assessment Checklist

Work through this quick checklist to validate your scores:

- [ ] Can you access your key business data from any device?
- [ ] Is your customer information in one place (not scattered across notebooks and inboxes)?
- [ ] Could a new team member follow your processes without being shown everything in person?
- [ ] Does your internet reliably support video calls and cloud tools?
- [ ] Has anyone on your team used ChatGPT, Claude, or a similar AI tool?
- [ ] Do you have a monthly budget — even a small one — for software subscriptions?
- [ ] Are your financial records digital and up to date?
- [ ] Do you back up your business data regularly?

If you ticked five or more, you're likely in the Emerging range or above. If you ticked fewer than three, start with the foundations — we'll cover exactly how in the next lessons.

## Key Takeaways

- **AI readiness has five dimensions**: Data Maturity (30%), Team Capability (25%), Process Documentation (20%), Infrastructure (15%), and Budget Alignment (10%)
- **Only 19% of NZ organisations are fully AI-ready** — you're not behind if you score low, you're normal
- **Data is the biggest factor** — if you only improve one thing, make it your data
- **Use the scoring system** to identify your weakest area and focus there first

# Auditing Your Data

Here's a secret that might surprise you: your small business is probably sitting on more useful data than you realise. Every invoice you've sent, every customer email you've received, every spreadsheet you've built — that's all data. And it's the fuel that makes AI work.

The problem isn't usually that you don't have enough data. It's that your data is messy, scattered, or locked away in places you've forgotten about. This lesson will help you figure out what you've got, how good it is, and what needs tidying up.

## Data You Already Have

Before you think about collecting new data, take stock of what's already there. Most small businesses have far more than they think.

| Source | What's In It | AI Potential |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Xero / MYOB** | Years of financial transactions, invoices, expenses | Cash flow forecasting, expense categorisation, tax prep |
| **Email inbox** | Customer conversations, supplier quotes, complaints | Customer sentiment analysis, auto-responses, lead tracking |
| **Spreadsheets** | Product lists, pricing, rosters, project tracking | Trend analysis, demand forecasting, reporting |
| **CRM / contact list** | Customer names, purchase history, preferences | Personalised marketing, churn prediction, segmentation |
| **Social media** | Engagement data, comments, follower demographics | Content optimisation, audience insights |
| **POS / Shopify** | Sales transactions, popular products, busy times | Inventory planning, pricing optimisation |
| **Google Analytics** | Website visitors, popular pages, traffic sources | Customer journey insights, content strategy |

> **Tip:** If you use Xero, you already have years of structured financial data ready to go. Xero's JAX AI assistant can immediately start working with your existing records — no data prep needed.

## Structured vs Unstructured Data

You'll hear these terms a lot in the AI world. Here's what they mean in plain English:

**Structured data** is information that fits neatly into rows and columns — like a spreadsheet or database. Your Xero transactions, customer contact list, or product inventory are all structured data. AI loves this stuff because it's already organised.

**Unstructured data** is everything else — emails, documents, images, social media posts, voice messages, PDFs. It's messy and doesn't fit into neat categories. Modern AI (especially generative AI) is actually brilliant at working with unstructured data, which is why it's suddenly so useful for small businesses.

> **Important:** You don't need to convert all your unstructured data into spreadsheets. Tools like ChatGPT and Claude can read documents, summarise emails, and extract information from messy data. That's literally what they're built for.

## The Four Qualities of Good Data

When AI experts talk about "data quality," they really mean four things:

### 1. Accurate
Is your data correct? If your customer database has old phone numbers, wrong email addresses, or duplicate entries, any AI tool working with it will produce unreliable results. Garbage in, garbage out.

### 2. Complete
Are there gaps? If half your customer records are missing email addresses, or your sales data only covers some of your products, AI won't have the full picture.

### 3. Consistent
Is the same information recorded the same way everywhere? If one spreadsheet lists "Auckland" and another uses "AKL" and a third says "Tāmaki Makaurau," that's an inconsistency that can confuse AI tools.

### 4. Accessible
Can you actually get to it when you need it? Data locked in someone's personal email, on a hard drive that's not backed up, or in a system nobody remembers the password to isn't useful to anyone — human or AI.

## Common Data Problems in Small Businesses

These are the issues we see most often. Sound familiar?

- **The spreadsheet jungle** — Multiple versions of the same spreadsheet, nobody knows which is current
- **The email archive** — Critical business information buried in thousands of emails
- **The paper pile** — Receipts, notes, and records that never made it into a digital system
- **The single point of failure** — Only one person knows where things are or how the system works
- **The mixed formats** — Some records in Word, some in Excel, some in Google Docs, some on paper
- **The duplicate problem** — Same customer entered three different ways across two systems

> **Warning:** If your business relies on one person's knowledge of where everything is stored, that's a risk — not just for AI, but for your entire business. What happens if they're sick, leave, or go on holiday?

## Your Quick Data Audit

Set aside 30 minutes and work through this checklist. You don't need to fix everything right now — just get a clear picture.

### Step 1: List Your Data Sources
Write down every system, tool, or place where your business stores information. Include:
- Accounting software (Xero, MYOB, Hnry)
- Email accounts
- Spreadsheets and documents (Google Drive, OneDrive, Dropbox)
- CRM or customer database
- Social media accounts
- Website analytics
- POS or e-commerce platform
- Paper files and notebooks

### Step 2: Rate Each Source
For each source, rate it 1–5 on accuracy, completeness, consistency, and accessibility.

### Step 3: Identify Your Quick Wins
Look for sources that score low but would be easy to improve. Common quick wins:
- Deduplicate your customer list
- Update contact information
- Move paper records into a digital system
- Consolidate multiple spreadsheets into one
- Set up automatic backups

### Step 4: Flag the Gaps
Note any critical business data that's missing entirely. For example:
- You track sales but not where customers heard about you
- You have financial records but no customer feedback data
- You know your revenue but not your profit by product or service

## Data Governance Basics

"Data governance" sounds corporate, but it's really just answering three questions:

1. **Who can access what?** Not everyone on your team needs access to everything. Financial data, customer personal information, and HR records should have restricted access.

2. **Where is it stored?** Have a clear system. Cloud storage with proper folder structures beats a random mix of local drives and USB sticks.

3. **How long do you keep it?** Under the Privacy Act 2020, you shouldn't hold personal information longer than you need it. Set some basic rules about when old data gets archived or deleted.

> **Tip:** Start simple. A shared Google Drive or OneDrive with clear folder names and basic access controls is a perfectly good foundation. You can get more sophisticated later.

## Key Takeaways

- **You already have useful data** — financial records, emails, customer information, and website analytics are all valuable to AI
- **Data quality matters more than data quantity** — focus on making your existing data accurate, complete, consistent, and accessible
- **Start with a 30-minute audit** — list your data sources, rate their quality, and identify quick wins
- **Basic data governance is essential** — know who can access what, where it's stored, and how long you keep it

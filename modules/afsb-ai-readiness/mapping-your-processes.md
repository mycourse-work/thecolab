# Mapping Your Processes

Every business has tasks that feel like Groundhog Day — the same emails, the same invoicing steps, the same social media routine, week after week. These repetitive tasks are exactly where AI can make the biggest difference. But before you can automate or improve anything, you need to see it clearly.

Process mapping sounds fancy, but it's really just writing down what you do and how long it takes. You don't need special software. A pen, some paper, and an honest look at your week is all it takes.

> **What you'll learn:** Map your repetitive business processes using a simple step-by-step method | Estimate the real time cost of your repetitive tasks per week and month | Identify which processes are the best candidates for AI assistance | Prioritise tasks based on frequency, time impact, and level of human judgement required

## Why Bother Mapping Your Processes?

When you run a small business, most processes live in your head. You just "know" how things get done. The problem is:

- You can't improve what you can't see
- You can't delegate what you can't describe
- You can't automate what you haven't documented
- AI tools need clear, repeatable tasks to work with

Mapping your processes gives you a clear picture of where your time actually goes — and where AI could give you hours back every week.

## Where to Look for Repetitive Tasks

Start with these common areas. Most small businesses have repetitive work hiding in all of them:

| Area | Common Repetitive Tasks |
|------|------------------------|
| **Admin** | Data entry, filing, scheduling, email sorting |
| **Invoicing & accounts** | Creating invoices, chasing payments, reconciling accounts, expense tracking |
| **Customer comms** | Responding to enquiries, booking confirmations, follow-up emails |
| **Social media** | Creating posts, scheduling content, responding to comments |
| **Reporting** | Weekly/monthly reports, KPI tracking, sales summaries |
| **HR & rosters** | Timesheets, leave requests, onboarding new team members |
| **Quoting** | Preparing quotes, sending proposals, following up |

## A Simple Process Mapping Method

You don't need Visio, Lucidchart, or any software. Here's a method that works with nothing but a piece of paper:

### Step 1: Pick a Task
Choose one repetitive task. Start with something you do at least weekly.

### Step 2: Write Down Every Step
Be specific. Don't write "send invoice." Write out the actual steps:
1. Open Xero
2. Find the customer
3. Enter the line items
4. Check the amounts
5. Add the due date
6. Preview and send
7. Log it in the tracking spreadsheet

### Step 3: Note the Time
Estimate how long each step takes and how often you do it. A task that takes 10 minutes but happens 20 times a week is over 3 hours — that's significant.

### Step 4: Mark the Pain Points
Which steps are boring, error-prone, or time-consuming? Put a star next to them. These are your automation candidates.

## Worked Example: A Small Consultancy's Weekly Admin

Let's walk through a real-world example. Sarah runs a small HR consultancy in Wellington with two staff.

**Task: Weekly client reporting (every Friday)**

| Step | Time | Pain Point? |
|------|------|-------------|
| Pull time data from Toggl | 10 min | |
| Copy data into spreadsheet | 15 min | Yes — manual and error-prone |
| Calculate hours by client | 10 min | Yes — formula always breaks |
| Write summary for each client | 30 min | Yes — repetitive writing |
| Format into email template | 15 min | |
| Send individually to 8 clients | 20 min | Yes — copy-paste job |
| **Total** | **1 hr 40 min** | |

**With AI assistance, Sarah's process could look like this:**

| Step | Time | How AI Helps |
|------|------|-------------|
| Export time data from Toggl | 5 min | Same as before |
| AI generates client summaries from data | 5 min | Claude or ChatGPT drafts personalised summaries |
| Review and tweak summaries | 10 min | Human check |
| Automated emails sent via template | 2 min | Email automation tool |
| **Total** | **22 min** | **Saving: 1 hr 18 min per week** |

That's over 5 hours a month Sarah gets back — from just one process.

## How to Estimate Your Time

People are terrible at guessing how long tasks take. Here are two ways to get a more accurate picture:

**Option 1: Time yourself for a week.** Use your phone timer or a free tool like Toggl. Track every repetitive task for five working days. You'll be surprised.

**Option 2: Use the "frequency x duration" formula.**

| Task | How Often | Minutes Each | Monthly Total |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Responding to enquiries | 5/day | 10 min | 16.5 hrs |
| Creating invoices | 10/week | 8 min | 5.3 hrs |
| Social media posts | 3/week | 20 min | 4 hrs |
| Weekly report | 1/week | 45 min | 3 hrs |
| Chasing payments | 3/week | 15 min | 3 hrs |
| **Total** | | | **31.8 hrs/month** |

> **Important:** If you're spending more than 10 hours a month on tasks that are repetitive and predictable, you've almost certainly got some strong candidates for AI assistance.

## Prioritising What to Tackle First

Not every process is worth automating. Use this simple priority system:

**Automate first:**
- High frequency (daily or more)
- Takes significant time per occurrence
- Doesn't require much human judgement
- Has clear, repeatable steps
- Errors have low consequences

**Automate later:**
- Lower frequency (weekly or monthly)
- Requires some human oversight
- Steps vary depending on context

**Keep human:**
- Requires relationship skills or empathy
- High-stakes decisions
- Creative or strategic thinking
- Complex negotiation

> **Warning:** Don't try to automate everything at once. Pick your single biggest time-waster and start there. Once that's working, move to the next one.

## Your Process Mapping Exercise

Set aside 20 minutes and do this right now:

1. **List your top 10 most repetitive tasks** (use the areas table above for inspiration)
2. **Estimate the time each one takes per week**
3. **Total it up** — this is your "automation opportunity" in hours per week
4. **Star your top 3** — the ones that are most repetitive, most time-consuming, and least enjoyable
5. **Pick one** to focus on as your first AI experiment

## Key Takeaways

- **Map your processes before you automate** — you can't improve what you can't see
- **Focus on repetitive, time-consuming, low-judgement tasks** — these are where AI delivers the biggest wins
- **You don't need fancy tools** — pen and paper process mapping works perfectly well
- **Start with one process** — get it working, then tackle the next one

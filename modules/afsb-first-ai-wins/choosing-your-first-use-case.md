# Choosing Your First Use Case

You've explored the tools, you understand the landscape, and you know your business is ready. Now it's time to actually do something with AI. But where do you start?

The biggest mistake people make is trying to do everything at once. The second biggest mistake is picking something too ambitious for a first go. In this lesson, we'll give you a framework for choosing the perfect first use case — something that gets you a quick win and builds your confidence.

## The Six Things AI Is Actually Good At

OpenAI studied over 300 real-world AI implementations and found that every successful use case fits into one of six categories — they call them **AI Primitives**:

1. **Content Creation** — Writing emails, drafting proposals, summarising documents, translating text
2. **Research** — Learning about topics, searching for information, analysing reports
3. **Coding** — Yes, even non-technical people can get AI to write simple scripts and formulas
4. **Data Analysis** — Making sense of spreadsheets, spotting trends, creating summaries
5. **Ideation & Strategy** — Brainstorming ideas, planning campaigns, thinking through problems
6. **Automation** — Streamlining repetitive, routine tasks

For most small business owners, **Content Creation** and **Research** are the easiest places to start. You're already writing emails, creating social posts, and looking things up — AI just makes those tasks faster.

Here's what each quadrant means:

- **Quick Wins** (high impact, low effort) — Do these first. They deliver results fast and build momentum. Think email drafting, social media content, meeting summaries.
- **Self-Service** (low impact, low effort) — Nice to have. Easy to set up but won't transform your business. Good for practice.
- **Strategic Projects** (high impact, high effort) — Worth doing eventually, but not where you start. Things like customer chatbots or inventory management systems.
- **Deferred** (low impact, high effort) — Leave these alone for now. Building a custom AI model when you could use ChatGPT? Park it.

> **Tip:** Your first AI project should always come from the Quick Wins quadrant. Get a win under your belt before tackling anything complex.

## How to Pick Your First Use Case

Ask yourself these four questions:

1. **What do I spend the most time on?** Look for tasks that eat up your hours — especially repetitive ones like writing similar emails, creating reports, or formatting documents.

2. **What's low-risk if it goes wrong?** Your first AI experiment shouldn't be something where a mistake costs you a client or creates a compliance issue. Internal documents are safer than client-facing ones while you're learning.

3. **What do I do frequently?** A task you do once a year isn't going to give you much practice. Pick something you do daily or weekly so you can iterate and improve quickly.

4. **What would save me the most headspace?** Sometimes it's not just about hours — it's about the mental load. If drafting your weekly newsletter stresses you out, that's a great candidate.

## Great First Use Cases for NZ Small Business

Here are some tried-and-true starting points:

- **Drafting customer emails** — Paste in the context, get a professional reply in seconds
- **Summarising meeting notes** — Turn rambling notes into structured action items
- **Creating social media posts** — Generate a week's worth of content in one sitting
- **Analysing spreadsheet data** — Upload a CSV and ask "what are the trends?"
- **Writing job descriptions** — Get a solid first draft in the right tone
- **Rewriting website copy** — Freshen up your About page or product descriptions
- **Brainstorming business ideas** — Use AI as a sounding board for strategy

> **Important:** You don't need to use AI for everything. Pick ONE thing, get good at it, then expand. The businesses that succeed with AI are the ones that start small and build up.

## Your Action Step

Before you move to the next lesson, make a decision: **What is the one task you'll use AI for this week?**

Write it down. Be specific. Not "I'll try AI" but "I'll use ChatGPT to draft my three follow-up emails on Tuesday afternoon."

The next lesson will teach you how to talk to AI effectively so you get genuinely useful results — not generic rubbish.

## Key Takeaways

- Every successful AI use case fits into one of six categories: Content Creation, Research, Coding, Data Analysis, Ideation & Strategy, or Automation
- Use the Impact/Effort Matrix to prioritise — start with Quick Wins (high impact, low effort)
- Your first use case should be low-risk, high-frequency, and something you already spend time on
- Pick ONE specific task to try this week — don't try to transform your entire business overnight
- Content Creation and Research are the easiest starting points for most small businesses

# AI for Marketing Your Business

Marketing is one of the biggest time sinks for small business owners. You know you should be posting on social media, sending newsletters, and keeping your website fresh — but who has the time? This is where AI genuinely shines. It won't replace your knowledge of your customers, but it will take the blank-page problem off the table.

Let's walk through the practical marketing tasks you can hand to AI today.

## Social Media Content

**The opportunity:** Most small businesses know they should post consistently but struggle to come up with content ideas and write posts regularly. AI can generate a week's worth of posts in about 10 minutes.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
Act as a social media manager for a NZ physiotherapy clinic in
Wellington called "Capital Physio". Our clients are mostly office
workers and weekend sports players aged 25-50.

Create 5 Facebook/Instagram posts for next week:
- 1 educational post (injury prevention tip)
- 1 promotional post (our ACC-registered services)
- 1 engagement post (question or poll)
- 1 behind-the-scenes post (humanise the team)
- 1 seasonal post (relevant to the current time of year)

Each post: under 150 words, include 3-5 relevant hashtags, and
suggest what type of image or photo would work well with it.
Tone: friendly, professional, approachable. NZ English.
```

**What you'll get:** Five ready-to-review posts with hashtags and image suggestions. Edit them to add your personal touch, swap in real team names, and schedule them using a free tool like Buffer.

**Time saved:** 1-2 hours per week on content creation.

## Blog Posts and Newsletters

**The opportunity:** Long-form content drives traffic and builds trust, but writing a 600-word blog post can take half a day when you're starting from scratch. AI gets you to the 80% mark fast.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
Write a blog post outline for a NZ accounting firm's website.
Topic: "5 Tax Deadlines NZ Small Business Owners Can't Afford
to Miss This Year"

For each deadline, include:
- The date
- What needs to be filed
- The penalty for missing it
- A practical tip for staying on track

Then draft the full post (600-800 words) in a conversational
but professional tone. Assume the reader is a small business
owner, not an accountant. Use NZ English and reference IRD
(not IRS).
```

> **Tip:** Use the few-shot technique from the Prompt Engineering lesson here. Paste in a previous blog post or newsletter you've written and tell the AI "Match this style and tone." Your content will sound like you, not like a robot.

**For newsletters**, try:
```text
I'm writing a monthly email newsletter for my customers. This
month I want to cover:
- Our new summer menu launch
- A staff spotlight on our new chef
- A reminder about our loyalty programme
- A community event we're sponsoring

Write the newsletter in a warm, personal tone. Keep it under
400 words total. Include subject line suggestions. Format for
email (short paragraphs, clear sections).
```

## Product Descriptions

**The opportunity:** If you sell products online, writing unique descriptions for each item is tedious. AI can batch-create them in your brand voice.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
Write product descriptions for an NZ online store selling
handmade skincare. Each description should be 40-60 words,
mention the key ingredients, and include one benefit statement.
Tone: natural, premium, approachable. NZ English.

Products:
1. Manuka Honey Face Mask (manuka honey, kaolin clay, aloe vera)
2. Kawakawa Healing Balm (kawakawa leaf, beeswax, coconut oil)
3. Pohutukawa Body Scrub (sea salt, pohutukawa extract, sweet almond oil)
```

**Time saved:** Writing 10-20 product descriptions manually might take 2-3 hours. With AI, you can do the same in 15-20 minutes including review and edits.

## Market Research

**The opportunity:** Understanding your market used to mean expensive reports or guesswork. AI can give you a solid starting point for customer insights and competitive analysis.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
What are the top 5 concerns and priorities for first-home
buyers in New Zealand right now? I run a small mortgage
advisory business. For each concern, suggest how I could
address it in my marketing content.
```

```text
Analyse the competitive landscape for independent coffee
roasters in Christchurch, NZ. What are the main ways small
roasters differentiate themselves? What marketing channels
work best for this type of business?
```

> **Warning:** AI's knowledge has a training cutoff date and it can present outdated information confidently. Always verify specific market data, statistics, and competitor details. Use AI for brainstorming and direction — not as your sole source of market intelligence.

## Image Creation

**The opportunity:** Professional-looking graphics used to require a designer. Tools like **Canva AI (Magic Studio)** let you create social media graphics, marketing materials, and presentations with AI assistance — from around $15/month for Pro.

Practical ways to use Canva AI:

- **Magic Design** — Describe what you want, get template suggestions
- **Magic Write** — Generate text for your designs
- **Magic Eraser** — Remove unwanted elements from photos
- **Background Remover** — Clean product photos for your online store
- **Text to Image** — Generate custom graphics from descriptions

For example: "Create a Facebook banner for a NZ cafe's summer drinks promotion, bright colours, featuring iced coffee" gives you a starting point you can customise with your branding.

## Video Creation

**The opportunity:** Short videos are among the most effective content on social media, but filming and editing takes time and confidence. AI tools like **HeyGen** (from $24/month) let you create simple promotional videos using AI avatars.

Practical uses:

- Product explainer videos for your website
- Welcome messages for new customers
- Short educational clips for social media
- Internal training videos for staff

You provide the script, choose an avatar and voice, and HeyGen produces a polished video. It's not going to win any film awards, but for a quick social media clip or website welcome video, it does the job.

## Keeping It Authentic

Here's the most important marketing lesson in this entire module: **AI should enhance your voice, not replace it.**

Your customers chose your business because of you — your personality, your expertise, your local knowledge. AI-generated content that sounds generic and corporate will actually hurt your brand. Follow these rules:

1. **Always review and edit.** Never post AI content without reading it through and adding your personal touches.
2. **Add real examples and stories.** AI can't tell your customers about the time Mrs. Henderson's dog ate the invoice. Those personal details are what make your content memorable.
3. **Check the facts.** AI can confidently state things that aren't true. Verify any specific claims, prices, dates, or statistics.
4. **Specify your audience.** Always include "Write this for a New Zealand audience, avoid American English" in your prompts. Nobody wants to read about "fall sales" in September.
5. **Use AI as a starting point, not the finish line.** The best content is AI-drafted, human-refined.

> **Important:** Under NZ's Fair Trading Act, your marketing must not be misleading or deceptive. If AI generates a claim about your product or service, you're responsible for making sure it's accurate. The penalties are significant — fines up to $600,000, rising to $5 million from 2026.

## Key Takeaways

- AI can generate a week's worth of social media content in 10-15 minutes — but always add your personal touch
- Use few-shot prompting (providing an example) to maintain your brand voice across all content
- Batch-create product descriptions, blog outlines, and newsletters to save hours each week
- Canva AI and HeyGen bring professional design and video creation within reach of any small business
- Always specify "NZ English" and "New Zealand audience" to avoid American-sounding content
- AI-generated marketing content is your responsibility under the Fair Trading Act — always verify claims before publishing

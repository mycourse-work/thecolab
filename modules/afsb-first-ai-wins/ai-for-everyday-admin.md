# AI for Everyday Admin

Admin is the stuff that keeps your business running but rarely moves it forward. Invoices, emails, meeting notes, policies, scheduling — it all adds up. Research from the AI Forum found that 93% of NZ workers using AI report efficiency gains, and admin tasks are where most people notice the difference first.

This lesson is hands-on. For each task, we'll give you a specific prompt you can try right now, show you what good output looks like, and estimate how much time you'll save.

## Email Drafting

**The problem:** You spend 30-60 minutes a day writing and replying to emails, many of which follow similar patterns.

**The AI approach:** Paste in the context or a brief, and get a professional draft in seconds.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
I need to reply to a supplier who has increased their prices by 12%.
I want to negotiate a smaller increase. Our relationship is good —
we've been with them for 4 years. Keep the tone professional but
firm. I'd like to suggest a meeting to discuss. Use NZ English.
```

**What good output looks like:** A complete, well-structured email that acknowledges the price increase, references your long-standing relationship, expresses concern about the impact on your business, and proposes a meeting to find a middle ground. Ready to review, personalise, and send.

**Time saved:** 10-15 minutes per email. If you write 5 similar emails a week, that's over an hour back.

> **Tip:** If you use Microsoft 365, Copilot can draft emails directly inside Outlook. You don't even need to switch to a separate tool — just describe what you want to say and it generates a draft in your email composer.

## Meeting Summaries

**The problem:** You leave a meeting with messy notes and good intentions, but turning those into clear action items never quite happens.

**The AI approach:** Paste your raw notes (even bullet points or sentence fragments) and get a structured summary.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
Here are my rough notes from today's team meeting. Create a
structured summary with these sections:
1. Key Decisions
2. Action Items (include who's responsible and deadline)
3. Items Needing Follow-Up
4. Parking Lot (things we mentioned but didn't resolve)

Notes:
- talked about new website launch, Sarah to get quotes by Friday
- need to sort the stockroom before Christmas rush
- Jake mentioned supplier issues with packaging
- agreed to move to fortnightly team meetings starting Feb
- budget for Q1 marketing still needs sign-off from Maria
- everyone liked the idea of a staff lunch for end of year
```

**What good output looks like:** A clean, scannable summary you can paste straight into an email to your team or drop into your project management tool.

**Time saved:** 15-20 minutes per meeting. For a business running 3-4 meetings a week, that's over an hour of admin eliminated.

## Document Creation

**The problem:** Writing policies, proposals, contracts, and standard operating procedures from scratch takes hours — and often gets pushed to the bottom of the to-do list.

**The AI approach:** Get a solid first draft in minutes, then refine with your specific details.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
Write a first draft of a return and refund policy for a small NZ
online retail store selling homewares. Include sections on:
- Timeframe for returns (I want 30 days)
- Condition of items for return
- Refund process and timeline
- Exclusions (sale items, personalised products)
- How to initiate a return
Keep it clear and customer-friendly. Reference the Consumer
Guarantees Act where relevant. Use NZ English.
```

**What good output looks like:** A complete, clearly structured policy document that covers all the essentials. It won't replace legal advice for complex matters, but it gives you a 90% complete draft that would have taken hours to write from scratch.

**Time saved:** 2-4 hours per document. For things like staff handbooks, health and safety procedures, or onboarding guides, this is a game-changer.

> **Important:** Always have a professional review AI-drafted legal or compliance documents before you publish them. AI gives you the starting structure — your accountant, lawyer, or HR adviser gives you the peace of mind.

## Data Entry and Cleanup

**The problem:** You've got a messy spreadsheet with inconsistent formatting, duplicates, or data that needs restructuring.

**The AI approach:** Paste the messy data into ChatGPT or Claude and ask it to clean, categorise, or restructure it.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
Here's a list of customer contacts exported from our old system.
Please:
1. Standardise the phone numbers to NZ format (+64...)
2. Fix any obvious typos in city names
3. Flag any duplicate entries
4. Organise by city alphabetically
5. Output as a table I can paste back into a spreadsheet

[Paste your data here]
```

**What good output looks like:** A clean, consistently formatted table ready to paste into Excel or Google Sheets. Duplicates flagged, phone numbers standardised, cities corrected.

**Time saved:** 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on the dataset. Tasks that would take an afternoon of tedious manual work can be done in minutes.

## Scheduling and Planning

**The problem:** Planning your week, organising project timelines, or figuring out task priorities takes mental energy you'd rather spend on actual work.

**The AI approach:** Describe your situation and constraints, and let AI create a structured plan.

**Try this prompt:**
```text
I run a small renovation business with 3 staff. This week I need to:
- Finish the bathroom job in Grey Lynn (2 days work remaining)
- Start a kitchen quote visit in Mt Eden (half day)
- Do the monthly accounts (I usually block 3 hours)
- Follow up with 4 suppliers about late deliveries
- Prepare a quote for a new deck build

Create a weekly schedule (Monday-Friday) that makes sense.
Assume 8-hour days. Put the most urgent client work first.
Block supplier calls into one session. Give me buffer time for
unexpected issues.
```

**What good output looks like:** A realistic day-by-day schedule with time blocks, logical grouping of similar tasks, and built-in buffer. Something you can transfer straight to your calendar.

**Time saved:** 20-30 minutes of planning time. More importantly, it reduces the mental load of figuring out how to fit everything in.

## Putting It All Together

The key with admin tasks is that none of them are individually transformative. But added up, they make a real difference. If AI saves you just 30 minutes a day on admin, that's **2.5 hours a week** — over **120 hours a year** — that you can spend on growing your business, serving customers, or simply getting home earlier.

Start with one of these tasks today. Literally right now, while you're thinking about it. Open ChatGPT or Claude, pick a prompt from above, and try it with your own real data.

> **Tip:** Save your best prompts somewhere handy — a note on your phone, a document in your shared drive, or even a sticky note on your monitor. The prompts you reuse weekly become your personal AI toolkit.

## Key Takeaways

- AI excels at repetitive admin tasks: emails, meeting summaries, document drafting, data cleanup, and scheduling
- For each task, provide clear context, specify the format you want, and always review the output before using it
- Even small time savings (10-15 minutes per task) compound into hours per week and days per year
- Microsoft Copilot users can access AI directly inside Outlook, Word, and Teams without switching tools
- Always have a professional review AI-generated legal or compliance documents
- Start with one admin task today — the best way to learn is by doing

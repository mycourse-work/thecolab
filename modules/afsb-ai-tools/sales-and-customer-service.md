# Sales, CRM, and Customer Service

For most small businesses, sales and customer service are deeply personal. You know your customers, you answer the phone yourself, and you pride yourself on the relationship. So where does AI fit in?

The answer isn't replacing that personal touch — it's handling the stuff that gets in the way of it. Logging customer details, following up on leads, answering the same questions for the hundredth time, and making sure nothing falls through the cracks.

## CRM — Keeping Track of Your Customers

A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system is where you track leads, customers, and sales opportunities. AI-powered CRMs do the tedious parts for you.

### HubSpot

HubSpot is one of the most popular CRMs globally, and its free plan is genuinely useful for small businesses.

| Plan | Price | What You Get |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | Contact management, email tracking, basic forms |
| Starter | ~$20/seat/month | AI lead scoring, email automation, reporting |
| Professional | ~$100/seat/month | Full automation, custom reporting, AI content |

**AI features:**
- **Lead scoring** — AI ranks your leads by likelihood to buy, so you focus on the best prospects
- **Email suggestions** — AI drafts follow-up emails based on conversation history
- **Meeting scheduling** — automated booking links that sync with your calendar
- **Chatbot builder** — create AI-powered chat for your website

**Who it's for:** Businesses with a sales pipeline — whether that's quoting jobs, following up enquiries, or managing repeat customers. The free plan handles the basics; the Starter plan adds the AI smarts.

### Zoho CRM (Zia AI)

Zoho CRM with its AI assistant Zia is the most affordable AI-enabled CRM on the market. It's a solid option for price-conscious businesses that still want AI features.

**What Zia does:**
- Predicts which deals will close
- Suggests the best time to contact leads
- Detects anomalies in sales data
- Automates data entry from emails and calls

**Who it's for:** Businesses that want CRM with AI but find HubSpot's paid plans too expensive.

## AI Chatbots and Customer Service

This is where AI can have an immediate, measurable impact on your business. AI chatbots handle routine customer queries 24/7, freeing you up to deal with the complex stuff.

### Tidio with Lyro AI

Tidio is a customer service platform with Lyro, an AI chatbot that learns from your existing content (FAQs, help articles, product pages) and handles customer questions automatically.

**The numbers:** Lyro can handle up to 64% of customer inquiries without human involvement. That's nearly two-thirds of your customer service workload, running 24/7 with no sick days or annual leave.

**How it works:**
1. Connect Tidio to your website
2. Feed it your FAQs, product information, and common questions
3. Lyro learns from this content and handles matching queries
4. Complex or unusual questions get passed to you

### aichatbot.nz

If you want a New Zealand-focused chatbot provider, aichatbot.nz specialises in building AI chatbots for Kiwi businesses. The advantage? They understand the NZ market, can handle NZ-specific queries, and offer local support.

### Shopify Inbox

If you run a Shopify store, Shopify Inbox provides free AI-powered chat. It can answer product questions, check order status, and recommend products — all included in your existing Shopify subscription.

## NZ Chatbot Success Stories

Two great examples from the NZ tourism sector show what's possible:

**Bolton Hotel — "Bea"**
The Bolton Hotel in Wellington launched an AI chatbot named Bea that handles booking enquiries, room information, local recommendations, and general questions. It provides 24/7 customer service without needing additional staff for after-hours queries.

**Cardrona Alpine Resort — "Pengi"**
Cardrona created Pengi to handle the avalanche of repetitive questions every ski resort gets: "Is the mountain open?", "What's the snow like?", "Where do I park?". Instead of staff answering the same questions hundreds of times a day, Pengi handles it instantly.

> **Tip:** These businesses didn't replace their customer service teams — they freed them up. Staff now spend time on complex queries and personal interactions rather than answering "What time do you open?" for the fiftieth time today.

## When Chatbots Make Sense (and When They Don't)

Chatbots are brilliant for:
- **FAQs** — opening hours, pricing, policies, directions
- **Order tracking** — "Where's my order?"
- **Booking enquiries** — availability, pricing, scheduling
- **Product information** — specs, availability, comparisons
- **After-hours cover** — capturing enquiries when you're closed

Chatbots are NOT a good fit for:
- **Complaints** — people want a real human when they're upset
- **Complex technical support** — nuanced problems need nuanced answers
- **High-value sales conversations** — big purchases need the personal touch
- **Sensitive matters** — health, legal, or financial advice should involve a human

> **Warning:** Under the Fair Trading Act, your chatbot's responses need to be accurate. If it tells a customer your product does something it doesn't, that's potentially a misleading claim — and the penalty for misleading conduct is increasing to up to $5 million from 2026. Make sure your chatbot's knowledge base is accurate and up to date.

## Customer Expectations Around AI Chat

Research shows that customers are increasingly comfortable with AI chat, provided:
- They know they're talking to a bot (be transparent)
- There's a clear option to reach a human
- The bot actually solves their problem
- Response times are fast

The worst thing you can do is deploy a chatbot that goes in circles, can't answer basic questions, and makes it hard to reach a real person. That's worse than no chatbot at all.

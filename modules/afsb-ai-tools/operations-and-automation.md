# Operations and Automation

This is where AI stops being a nice-to-have and starts saving you real money. Operational tasks — data entry, chasing invoices, managing inventory, processing paperwork — eat up hours every week. Most of them follow predictable patterns, which makes them perfect for automation.

The question isn't whether to automate. It's what to automate first.

## No-Code Workflow Automation

### Make.com

Make.com (formerly Integrobot) lets you connect your business apps and automate workflows without writing a single line of code. Think of it as building a chain: when this happens in App A, do this in App B.

**Examples of what you can automate:**
- New enquiry comes in via your website form → automatically added to your CRM → sends you a notification → triggers a welcome email
- Invoice marked as paid in Xero → updates your project management tool → sends a thank-you email to the client
- Social media post published → automatically shared across other platforms → logged in a tracking spreadsheet

| Plan | Price | What You Get |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | 1,000 operations/month, basic integrations |
| Core | ~$9/month | 10,000 operations/month, more integrations |
| Pro | ~$16/month | Unlimited scenarios, priority support |

**Who it's for:** Any business doing repetitive tasks across multiple apps. If you find yourself copying information from one system to another, Make.com can probably do it for you.

> **Tip:** Start by writing down every task you do that involves copying information between systems. That list is your automation hit list. Pick the most time-consuming one and build your first Make.com scenario.

## Finance and Admin Automation

### Dext — Receipt and Invoice Processing

Dext uses AI to read your receipts, invoices, and bills — extracting supplier names, amounts, dates, and GST — then pushes the data straight into Xero or MYOB.

**How it works:**
1. Snap a photo of a receipt or forward an invoice email to your Dext address
2. AI extracts all the relevant data
3. Data gets categorised and pushed to your accounting software
4. You approve and it's done

**Why it matters:** If you're spending time manually entering receipts and invoices, Dext can save hours every month. For trades businesses with stacks of supplier invoices and receipts from Bunnings, this is a no-brainer.

## Automation vs Augmentation

Not everything should be fully automated. Here's a practical framework:

**Fully automate** when:
- The task is repetitive and rule-based (data entry, notifications, filing)
- Mistakes have low consequences (social media scheduling, report generation)
- Speed matters more than judgment (order confirmations, receipt processing)

**Augment (AI assists, human decides)** when:
- The task requires judgment (hiring decisions, customer complaints)
- Mistakes have high consequences (financial advice, legal compliance)
- Personal relationships matter (sales conversations, client management)
- Creativity is important (brand strategy, product development)

> **Important:** The goal isn't to remove humans from every process. It's to remove the boring, repetitive parts so humans can focus on the work that actually needs a brain. The best results come from AI and people working together, not AI replacing people.

## Building Your Tool Stack

Start small and build up. Here's a sensible progression:

**Month 1:** Try free tiers. Get comfortable with one AI assistant. Turn on AI features in your existing software (Xero, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365).

**Month 2-3:** Add one paid tool that addresses your biggest time sink. For most businesses, that's either Canva Pro (for marketing) or a CRM like HubSpot (for sales).

**Month 3-6:** Look at automation. Connect your tools with Make.com. Consider industry-specific tools. Evaluate whether a chatbot makes sense for your customer service.

**Ongoing:** Review what's working, drop what isn't, and keep an eye on new features in your existing tools.

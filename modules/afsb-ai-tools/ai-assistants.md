# AI Assistants: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot

If you only do one thing after this course, it should be this: try an AI assistant. These are the "Swiss Army knives" of artificial intelligence — general-purpose tools that can help you write emails, summarise documents, brainstorm ideas, analyse data, and much more.

The good news? You can start for free, right now. The even better news? You're probably already paying for one without realising it.

## The Big Four

There are four major AI assistants worth knowing about. Each has a free option, and each has its own strengths. Here's how they stack up:

| Feature | ChatGPT (OpenAI) | Claude (Anthropic) | Gemini (Google) | Microsoft Copilot |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Free tier** | Yes — limited | Yes — limited | Yes — basic | Yes — basic |
| **Paid price** | Plus $20/mo, Business $25/seat/mo | Pro $17–20/mo, Team $25–30/person/mo | Included in Workspace ($14/user/mo) | $21/user/mo (with Microsoft 365) |
| **Best for** | General tasks, coding, image generation | Long documents, analysis, careful writing | Google ecosystem users | Microsoft 365 users |
| **NZ considerations** | Most widely known, huge plugin ecosystem | Growing fast, strong on accuracy | Bundled with Google Workspace — you may already have it | NZ pilot showed 1,781% ROI |

## ChatGPT — The Household Name

ChatGPT is the tool that started the AI conversation. It's the most widely recognised AI assistant and has the largest ecosystem of integrations and plugins.

**What it's good at:** General writing, brainstorming, coding help, image generation (DALL-E), and working with uploaded files. The Plus plan gives you access to the latest models and features.

**Who it's best for:** Businesses that want the broadest range of features and don't mind paying for premium access. It's also a safe starting point because there's so much community knowledge and tutorials available.

> **Tip:** The free version of ChatGPT is genuinely useful. Start there before deciding whether the $20/month Plus plan is worth it for your business.

## Claude — The Thoughtful Analyst

Claude (made by Anthropic) has earned a reputation for careful, nuanced responses. It handles long documents exceptionally well and tends to be more measured in its answers — less likely to make things up.

**What it's good at:** Analysing lengthy documents, detailed writing, summarising reports, and tasks requiring careful reasoning. It can process very large files in one go, making it great for reviewing contracts, financial reports, or policy documents.

**Who it's best for:** Professional services, businesses dealing with complex documents, and anyone who values accuracy over speed. If you're a consultant, accountant, or lawyer, Claude is worth a serious look.

## Gemini — Already in Your Google Account

If your business runs on Google Workspace (Gmail, Google Docs, Sheets, Drive), Gemini is built right in. Google dropped the price of its AI-enabled Workspace plans to $14/user/month, making this one of the most cost-effective options.

**What it's good at:** Working directly inside your Google apps — drafting emails in Gmail, creating content in Docs, analysing data in Sheets, and organising files in Drive. The integration is seamless because it already knows your Google data.

**Who it's best for:** Any business already on Google Workspace. If you're paying for Google already, check whether Gemini is included in your plan — it might be just a settings toggle away.

> **Tip:** If you're already on Google Workspace, log into admin.google.com and check your plan. You might already have Gemini access without realising it.

## Microsoft Copilot — The Office Powerhouse

Microsoft Copilot lives inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. A New Zealand pilot programme showed a 1,781% return on investment, with users saving an average of 13 hours per month.

**What it's good at:** Drafting documents in Word, creating presentations in PowerPoint, analysing spreadsheets in Excel, summarising email threads in Outlook, and generating meeting notes in Teams.

**Who it's best for:** Businesses running Microsoft 365. At $21/user/month on top of your existing Microsoft subscription, it's not cheap — but those time savings can be significant if you live in the Microsoft ecosystem.

> **Important:** Copilot works best when your Microsoft 365 data is well-organised. If your SharePoint and OneDrive are a mess, clean them up first — Copilot will search through everything, including files you'd rather it didn't find.

## Which One Should You Start With?

Don't overthink this. The best AI assistant is the one you'll actually use. Here's a simple decision tree:

1. **Already on Google Workspace?** Start with Gemini — it's likely included in your plan.
2. **Already on Microsoft 365?** Try the free Copilot features first, then consider the paid upgrade.
3. **Not tied to either ecosystem?** Start with the free tiers of ChatGPT and Claude. Use both for a week and see which one clicks.
4. **On a tight budget?** All four have free tiers. Use them. You can get real value without spending a cent.

According to the AI Forum of NZ, 72% of New Zealand businesses favour off-the-shelf AI solutions over custom-built ones. These four assistants are exactly that — ready to use, no technical setup required.

## What You Can Do This Week

Pick one AI assistant and try these five tasks:

- **Draft an email** you've been putting off
- **Summarise a long document** (upload a PDF and ask for key points)
- **Brainstorm ideas** for your next marketing campaign
- **Write a job description** for a role you're hiring
- **Create a social media post** about your business

You'll be surprised how quickly these tools become part of your daily routine. The trick is to start using them for real work — not just playing around.

## Key Takeaways

- All four major AI assistants offer free tiers — there's no reason not to try them today
- Choose based on what software you already use (Google, Microsoft, or neither)
- The NZ Microsoft Copilot pilot showed 1,781% ROI and 13 hours saved per month
- 72% of NZ businesses prefer off-the-shelf AI tools, and these assistants are exactly that

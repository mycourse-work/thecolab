# Vendor Assessment and Procurement

Choosing an AI tool isn't like choosing a new stapler. When you sign up for an AI platform, you're trusting it with your business data, your customer information, and potentially your reputation. This lesson gives you a practical framework for evaluating AI vendors — so you make informed choices, not impulse ones.

MBIE published an AI Procurement Checklist as part of its Responsible AI Guidance for Business (July 2025). We'll use that as our foundation and adapt it for small business reality.

## The Questions That Matter

Before you commit to any AI tool, ask these questions. You won't always get perfect answers, but the way a vendor responds tells you a lot about whether you can trust them.

**Where is my data stored and processed?**
Most AI tools process data in the US or EU. That's not automatically a problem — but you need to know, especially under the Privacy Act's cross-border transfer rules. Some tools now offer processing in the AWS NZ region.

**Who can access my data?**
Can the vendor's employees see your data? Is it shared with third parties? Is it used to train AI models? Business-tier accounts usually have clearer boundaries here than free tiers.

**Can I export my data?**
If you decide to leave the platform, can you take your data with you? Tools that lock you in make it hard and expensive to switch. Look for export functionality.

**What happens if the tool shuts down?**
AI is a fast-moving market. Companies come and go. Soul Machines raised over $225 million and still ended up in receivership. What's the vendor's plan for data if the service closes? Will you get notice? Will your data be deleted?

**How is the AI model updated?**
AI tools improve over time — but updates can also change how they behave. Ask whether you'll be notified about significant model changes, and whether you can test updates before they go live.

**What's the uptime guarantee?**
If you depend on the tool for daily operations, you need to know their reliability commitment. Look for a Service Level Agreement (SLA) with uptime percentages and compensation terms.

## Terms of Service Red Flags

Before you click "I agree," actually read the terms. Here's what to watch for:

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| **Your data is used to train the AI model** | Your customer info and business data could end up influencing the AI's responses to other users. Common in free tiers. |
| **Unclear data ownership** | The terms should clearly state that you own your data and your outputs. If this is vague, ask for clarification in writing. |
| **No data deletion guarantee** | When you delete data or close your account, it should actually be deleted — not just hidden. Look for clear deletion timelines. |
| **Broad rights to your content** | Some terms grant the vendor wide-ranging rights to use, modify, and distribute your content. Read the "license you grant us" section carefully. |
| **Unilateral terms changes** | Can the vendor change terms at any time without notice? Better vendors commit to advance notice of material changes. |
| **No liability for AI outputs** | Almost all AI tools disclaim liability for their outputs — that's standard. But understand that this means YOU are responsible for checking and using AI outputs appropriately. |

> **Tip:** If the terms of service are thousands of words of dense legalese, try pasting them into an AI tool (ironic, but practical) and asking for a plain-English summary of key risks. Just don't paste anything confidential to do it.

## Evaluating AI Vendors: A Practical Scorecard

Use this scorecard when comparing AI tools. Rate each criterion from 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent). A tool doesn't need to score 5 across the board — but a score of 1 or 2 on data protection or reliability should make you think twice.

| Criterion | Questions to Ask | Score (1-5) |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Data protection** | Where is data stored? Is it used for training? Is there a data processing agreement? | |
| **NZ relevance** | Does the tool work well for NZ businesses? NZ-based references? NZ data residency option? | |
| **Reliability** | What's the uptime SLA? How often are there outages? | |
| **Ease of use** | Can your team use it without extensive training? | |
| **Integration** | Does it connect with tools you already use (Xero, Google Workspace, etc.)? | |
| **Data portability** | Can you export your data? What formats? | |
| **Support** | NZ-hours support available? Response time commitments? | |
| **Pricing clarity** | Is pricing straightforward? Any hidden costs? What happens when you scale? | |
| **Track record** | How long has the company been operating? Is it financially stable? Customer reviews? | |
| **Exit plan** | What happens to your data if you leave or the company closes? | |

> **Ask for NZ references.** If a vendor is serious about the NZ market, they should be able to point you to NZ customers or case studies. If they can't, that's not necessarily a dealbreaker — but it tells you something about how well they understand our market.

## Build vs Buy

For the vast majority of small businesses, the answer is **buy**. Building custom AI requires technical expertise, time, and ongoing maintenance most small teams don't have. Off-the-shelf tools are almost always the right choice. Even if you have a unique need, start with an existing tool to validate the concept before investing in a custom build.

## Contract Considerations

For business or enterprise tier subscriptions, pay attention to these elements:

| Contract Element | What to Check |
|-----------------|---------------|
| **SLAs** | What uptime is guaranteed? Look for 99.5%+ for critical tools |
| **Data Processing Agreements** | How the vendor handles storage, access, deletion, and breach notification |
| **Exit clauses** | Lock-in period? Can you export data in a usable format? |
| **Renewal terms** | Auto-renewal at higher rates? Set a calendar reminder before renewal |
| **Price escalation** | Can prices increase mid-contract? By how much? |

## Free vs Paid: When to Upgrade

Free tiers are great for exploration and personal productivity. But for business use, there's usually a clear line where you should upgrade.

**Stay on free tier when:**
- You're just trying out the tool to see if it's useful
- You're using it for purely personal tasks with no business data
- The tool processes no customer or business data at all

**Upgrade to paid when:**
- You're using the tool regularly for business tasks
- Any customer or business data is involved
- You need the data protection of a business-tier agreement
- You want admin controls (manage team access, view usage)
- You need reliable access without usage caps
- You need the tool for customer-facing work

The cost difference is typically $15-30 per user per month. For a tool you use daily, that's an easy business case — less than a dollar a day for data protection, better features, and peace of mind.

## Your Vendor Assessment Process

Here's a simple process for evaluating a new AI tool before you commit:

1. **Identify the need.** What business problem does this tool solve? Is it a genuine need or a "nice to have"?
2. **Research options.** Compare at least two or three alternatives. Check NZ-focused review sites, ask other business owners, check the AI Forum NZ directory.
3. **Score with the vendor scorecard.** Rate each option against the criteria above.
4. **Read the terms of service.** Look for the red flags listed in this lesson.
5. **Start with a trial.** Most tools offer free trials of their paid tier. Test with non-sensitive data first.
6. **Make the decision.** Document why you chose this tool and add it to your AI register.
7. **Review after 90 days.** Is the tool delivering value? Are there any concerns? Adjust or switch if needed.

## Key Takeaways

- Always ask: Where is my data? Who can access it? Can I export it? What happens if the tool shuts down?
- Watch for terms of service red flags, especially around data training, ownership, and deletion.
- Use the vendor evaluation scorecard to compare options objectively. Ask for NZ references.
- For most small businesses, buying off-the-shelf AI tools beats building custom solutions.
- Upgrade from free to paid tiers as soon as you're using a tool with business or customer data.
- Pay attention to SLAs, data processing agreements, exit clauses, and renewal terms.
- Document your vendor selection in your AI register and review every 90 days initially, then at least every six months.

# The Art of the Nark: Receipts vs Vibes

If message karma is the Kingdom's steady income, **narking** is its high-stakes sport. This is the mechanic that gives narkcoin its name, and it's the one place in the whole system where a well-argued case can earn you real credits — or a lazy one can cost you dearly.

## What "filing a nark" means

To file a nark, you report another member (or, just as often, Clawd itself) for some perceived offence, and Clawd rules on it — in character, in public, immediately. This isn't a real moderation report; it's a comedic accountability game where the currency is credits and the judge is an AI with a strong sense of humour and a lower tolerance for nonsense than you might expect.

Every nark ruling comes down to one question: **does it have a receipt?**

> **Important:** A "receipt" just means actual evidence — a real quote, a real screenshot, a real named target, a specific and true claim. Vague vibes ("he's always doing it"), self-nominated targets with no offence on file, or narking yourself for attention all fail the receipt test, no matter how funny the delivery is.

## Good narks: rewarded, and rewarded to scale

When a nark is well-evidenced, Clawd awards the filer credits — and the amount isn't fixed. Weaker-but-valid narks tend to land around **+1**, while sharply argued, fully evidenced narks can earn as much as **+25**. The difference usually comes down to how many "boxes" the nark ticks:

- A **named target** (not "someone" or "a certain person")
- A concrete, checkable **claim** (not a vibe or a feeling)
- An actual **receipt** — a quote, a log, a screenshot, a real reference

Nail all three and you're looking at the top of the range. Nail one or two and you'll still get something, just less. Clawd has, on occasion, ruled in favour of narks filed *against the Treasury itself* — catching real inconsistencies in the bot's own ledger or rulings counts as a legitimate, evidence-backed nark like any other.

## Bad narks: fined, not just ignored

This is the part that keeps the game honest: a weak nark isn't merely rejected, it's **fined** — typically **-25 credits** straight to the filer. Common ways a nark gets marked "vibes-only" and fined:

- No named target at all
- Narking someone for something that isn't actually an offence (being good at your job doesn't count)
- Self-narks with no real basis — narking yourself isn't evidence, it's attention-seeking
- "Everyone knows he does this" style claims with zero receipt
- Reporting the Treasury for stinginess or incompetence *without* pointing at an actual logged mistake

The symmetry is the whole point: the system that rewards good detective work punishes lazy accusations by exactly the same mechanism, in the same public ledger, ruled by the same judge.

## Appealing a ruling

Disagree with a fine or a loss? That's what **`/appeal`** is for — it lets you contest your most recent loss, and the case goes straight to Karen. As covered in Module 1, Karen's rulings land in one of three ways:

1. **Upheld** — the original loss is reversed and credits are refunded
2. **Partially upheld** — some credits come back, not all of them
3. **Denied, or backfires** — the appeal fails, sometimes with an extra "court cost" fine on top

Appeals are themselves subject to the receipt test. Karen has, on the record, denied an appeal with the note "sir you didn't even TRY" — an appeal with no actual argument attached goes nowhere. She's also upheld appeals where a member genuinely dug up the "damp receipt under the printer," so to speak — real evidence, presented properly, does move her.

> **Tip:** The best narks and the best appeals share the same skill: state exactly what happened, name exactly who's involved, and back it with something checkable. It's a small, low-stakes way to practise making a clear, evidenced argument — which happens to be useful well beyond a joke currency.

## Big losses get remembered

There's one more flourish worth knowing: any loss of **250 credits or more** — whether from a fine, a bad bet, or the guillotine covered in the next module — triggers a tiny **Nark Credit obituary** in the channel. It's a small, theatrical touch that turns even your worst losses into shared entertainment rather than a private sting.

## Key Takeaways

- Filing a nark means publicly reporting a member (or Clawd itself) for an "offence," ruled on immediately and in character.
- Good, evidence-backed narks earn the filer +1 to +25 credits, scaled by how many of "named target, concrete claim, real receipt" the nark satisfies.
- Weak, vibes-only, or self-serving narks are fined — typically -25 credits — punishing lazy accusations through the same system that rewards good ones.
- `/appeal` lets you contest your latest loss to Karen, who can uphold, partially uphold, or deny (sometimes with an extra fine) based on whether your appeal itself has a receipt.
- Any loss of 250+ credits triggers a small public "obituary" message, turning big losses into shared entertainment.

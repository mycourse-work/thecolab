# Industry Trends for NZ

AI isn't hitting every industry at the same speed or in the same way. Some sectors in New Zealand are already seeing massive results, while others are just getting started — which means there's still plenty of room for early movers to gain an edge.

In this lesson, we'll look at what's happening across six key NZ sectors, what's coming next, and what you can do about it right now.

## Agriculture and Agritech

If there's one sector where NZ punches above its weight in AI, it's agriculture. The AI Forum has said that "AI is becoming the new fertiliser" — and the numbers back that up.

**What's happening now:**
- **Halter** has raised over $200M for its GPS-enabled smart collars. Their "Cowgorithm" algorithms deliver a 12% boost in milk yield and a 27% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions per litre. They've expanded to the US, Australia, and Europe.
- **Robotics Plus** is building autonomous hybrid vehicles for orchards and vineyards (Prospr), achieving 70% reduction in fuel consumption.
- **Figured** provides cloud-based farm accounting that integrates with Xero, giving farmers real-time financial visibility.
- **CropX** uses AI-powered soil sensing to optimise irrigation and fertiliser application.

**What's coming:**
Predictive analytics for animal health, autonomous harvesting, and AI-driven supply chain optimisation from farm to market. Expect AI to increasingly connect the dots between weather data, soil conditions, market prices, and farm operations.

**What to do now:** If you're in agriculture, look at tools that connect your farm data to your financial systems. Figured + Xero is a good starting point. For larger operations, explore precision agriculture tools like CropX.

## Tourism and Hospitality

Tourism has what the industry calls a "digital back office" gap — meaning most operators are still running on manual processes, spreadsheets, and gut feeling. That's actually great news if you're willing to move early.

**What's happening now:**
- **Bolton Hotel** uses chatbot "Bea" and **Cardrona Alpine Resort** uses "Pengi" for 24/7 customer service without additional staffing
- AI-powered booking management and dynamic pricing are becoming mainstream for larger operators
- Personalised marketing using AI to segment visitors and send targeted offers

**What's coming:**
AI concierge services that create personalised itineraries, predictive demand modelling for staffing and pricing, multilingual AI communication for international visitors, and AI-generated content for destination marketing.

**What to do now:** Start with an AI chatbot for your website to handle common questions (pricing, availability, directions). Tools like Tidio can handle up to 64% of enquiries automatically. Then look at AI for your social media content — tourism is a visual, story-driven industry and AI is brilliant at helping you tell those stories.

## Trades and Construction

The trades sector is adopting AI faster than many people expect, driven by practical tools that solve real problems.

**What's happening now:**
- **Simply Automated** is specifically targeting NZ tradies with AI tools for quoting and job management
- **Tradify** and **Fergus** (both NZ-built) are adding AI features for job scheduling, quoting, and customer communication
- AI estimating tools can analyse project specs and generate accurate quotes in minutes instead of hours

**What's coming:**
AI-powered project documentation (take photos on site, AI generates the report), predictive scheduling that accounts for weather and supply chain delays, automated compliance checking against building codes, and AI assistants that help apprentices access technical information on the job.

**What to do now:** If you're still quoting manually, explore AI-assisted estimating tools. If you're using Tradify or Fergus, make sure you're taking advantage of their AI features. For project documentation, try using ChatGPT or Claude to turn your site notes into professional reports.

## Professional Services

Legal, accounting, and consulting firms are seeing some of the most dramatic AI productivity gains in the country.

**What's happening now:**
- **LawHawk** delivers a 75% reduction in legal document preparation time
- **Law Cyborg** provides AI-powered legal research for NZ law
- **Xero JAX** is transforming accounting with AI-driven reconciliation, categorisation, payroll, and forecasting
- **MYOB** has added Smart Invoice Reminders, AI Business Insights, and AI Coding Suggestions
- **Momentum Consulting** freed a partial FTE in finance and reduced management Q&A time by 15%

**What's coming:**
AI-first professional services where routine work is handled by AI and humans focus on strategy, relationships, and complex judgment calls. Expect AI-powered contract review, automated compliance monitoring, and AI assistants that prepare client briefs and research memos.

**What to do now:** If you're not already using AI for document drafting and research, start there — it's the quickest win in professional services. Use Claude or ChatGPT to draft client emails, summarise documents, and research topics. If you're in law, explore LawHawk for document automation.

## Retail

Retail is being transformed by AI behind the scenes — in inventory management, demand forecasting, and waste reduction.

**What's happening now:**
- **Foodstuffs North Island** uses WhyWaste AI for real-time inventory tracking, achieving up to 90% reduction in food waste
- **Cin7** (NZ-founded) provides AI-powered inventory management
- **Shopify** includes AI features for product descriptions, customer communication, and marketing
- AI-driven personalised marketing is helping retailers target the right customers with the right offers

**What's coming:**
Hyper-personalised shopping experiences, AI-powered demand forecasting that accounts for weather, events, and trends, automated visual merchandising suggestions, and cashierless checkout powered by computer vision.

**What to do now:** If inventory management is a pain point, look at Cin7 or similar tools. If you're on Shopify, explore its built-in AI features. For marketing, use AI to create personalised email campaigns — even basic segmentation powered by AI can significantly boost conversion rates.

## Healthcare

Healthcare AI in NZ is still early but growing fast, with practical applications that any practice can adopt.

**What's happening now:**
- AI appointment scheduling and automated reminders are reducing no-shows
- AI-powered patient communication for after-hours queries
- Automated claims processing and billing
- AI scribes that transcribe and summarise consultation notes

**What's coming:**
AI-assisted clinical decision support, predictive health analytics, automated patient triage, and AI-powered population health management. Telehealth combined with AI will expand access to care in rural NZ.

**What to do now:** Start with AI for administrative tasks — scheduling, reminders, and patient communication. These don't involve clinical decision-making, so they're lower risk and deliver immediate time savings.

## Key Takeaways

- NZ's agritech sector leads in AI adoption, with companies like Halter and Robotics Plus building world-class solutions
- Tourism has a "digital back office" gap — early movers who adopt AI for customer service and marketing will gain a significant advantage
- Trades and construction are adopting AI faster than expected, with NZ-built tools like Tradify, Fergus, and Simply Automated leading the way
- Professional services see some of the biggest time savings, with LawHawk achieving 75% reduction in document preparation
- Retail AI is strongest behind the scenes — inventory management, waste reduction, and personalised marketing
- Regardless of your industry, the pattern is the same: start with administrative and operational tasks, then expand to customer-facing applications

# Staying Current and Connected

AI is moving fast. Really fast. New tools launch every week, capabilities improve every month, and what felt cutting-edge six months ago can feel basic today. That pace can be overwhelming — but it doesn't have to be.

The trick isn't to follow everything. It's to follow the right things, connect with the right people, and build a simple habit of staying informed. In New Zealand, we're lucky to have a strong network of organisations and resources specifically designed to help businesses like yours keep up.

## Your Go-To NZ Resources

### AI Forum NZ

**What it is:** The leading AI industry body in New Zealand, with 280+ member organisations.

**Why it matters:** They produce the research, frameworks, and tools that shape how NZ businesses adopt AI. Their free governance framework alone is worth your time.

**What you get:**
- Free AI Governance Framework at [aigovernance.aiforum.org.nz](https://aigovernance.aiforum.org.nz)
- Free e-learning modules on AI fundamentals and ethics at [aiforum.org.nz](https://aiforum.org.nz)
- AI Capability Directory — an interactive map of the NZ AI ecosystem
- Regular events, webinars, and networking opportunities
- AI Productivity Reports with NZ-specific adoption data

> **Start here:** If you do nothing else from this lesson, sign up for the AI Forum newsletter and bookmark their governance framework. It's the single best way to stay connected to the NZ AI ecosystem.

### Aotearoa AI Summit

**What it is:** The annual gathering of NZ's AI community — business leaders, researchers, policymakers, and practitioners.

**Why it matters:** It's the best place to see what's actually happening in NZ AI (not just what's happening overseas), hear from businesses at different stages of their AI journey, and make connections with people who can help you.

### NZTech

**What it is:** The parent organisation of the AI Forum, representing New Zealand's tech sector at [technewzealand.org.nz](https://technewzealand.org.nz).

**Why it matters:** Broader tech context helps you understand where AI fits into the wider digital transformation picture. They advocate for policy settings that support NZ businesses.

### NewZealand.AI

**What it is:** A platform featuring NZ-specific AI case studies, adoption insights, and training resources.

**Why it matters:** Real stories from real NZ businesses are more useful than Silicon Valley hype. Seeing how a Christchurch accounting firm or a Waikato farmer uses AI is far more relevant to your situation.

## Government and Business Support

### Business Mentors NZ

Published the "Plan Your 2026 Business Year With AI" guide — a practical resource for small business owners. They also offer free mentoring from experienced business people, some of whom now specialise in helping businesses adopt technology.

### Business.govt.nz

The government's small business portal. Increasingly featuring AI-related content, guides, and links to funding opportunities. A good one-stop shop for understanding what support is available.

### Regional Chambers of Commerce

Don't overlook your local chamber. MBIE is funding AI workshops through regional chambers — Tauranga's programme is a good example. These workshops are practical, local, and often free or low-cost.

> **Tip:** Contact your local chamber of commerce and ask what AI-related events or workshops they have planned. If they don't have any, suggest it — they're responsive to member demand.

### AI Advisory Pilot Programme

The government's $765,000 programme offering co-funding of up to $15,000 per business for AI roadmaps and implementation. Delivered through the Regional Business Partner Network. If you haven't explored this yet, go back to Module 6 for details.

## Online Communities

You don't need to attend events to stay connected. Online communities can be just as valuable:

- **LinkedIn NZ AI groups** — Search for "AI New Zealand" or "NZ AI" on LinkedIn. These groups share news, job postings, and discussion about AI in the NZ context.
- **AI Forum events** — Many are now hybrid (in-person and online), so you can attend from anywhere in the country.
- **Tech meetups** — Sites like Meetup.com have NZ-based AI and tech groups in most major cities.

## Staying Current Without Losing Your Mind

Here's the thing: you don't need to become an AI expert. You need to be informed enough to make good decisions for your business. Here's a simple system:

**Follow 2-3 sources (not 20)**

Pick two or three from the list above and commit to those. The AI Forum newsletter plus one LinkedIn group is plenty. Information overload leads to paralysis, not progress.

**Set aside 30 minutes per week**

Block out half an hour — maybe Friday morning with a coffee. Scan your chosen sources, read one article properly, and note anything relevant to your business. That's it.

**Try one new thing per month**

Not a new tool necessarily — maybe a new way of using an existing tool. "This month I'll try using Claude to draft my client proposals." Small experiments compound into real capability over time.

**Talk to one person per quarter**

Have a conversation with someone further along the AI journey than you. A fellow business owner, a consultant, someone from the AI Forum. One good conversation can save you months of trial and error.

## Don't Forget Security

Here's a stat that should focus your attention: **81% of NZ small businesses have experienced a cybersecurity breach**.

Staying current with AI isn't just about finding new tools and opportunities. It also means staying aware of security risks. As you adopt more AI tools:

- Make sure your team knows not to paste sensitive data into AI tools without checking the privacy policy
- Keep your AI tools updated — security patches matter
- Review your cyber insurance coverage (we covered this in Module 5)
- Follow CERT NZ for cybersecurity alerts relevant to NZ businesses
- Consider basic cybersecurity training for your team — many AI tools are accessed online, which expands your attack surface

> **Remember:** Every new tool you add is another potential entry point for a security issue. That's not a reason to avoid AI — it's a reason to adopt it thoughtfully, with basic security hygiene in place.

## Building Your Personal AI Radar

Here's a simple framework to keep yourself informed without it becoming another job:

| Frequency | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly** | Scan AI Forum newsletter or LinkedIn group | 15-30 min |
| **Monthly** | Try a new AI capability or tool feature | 30-60 min |
| **Quarterly** | Attend an event or have a conversation with an AI-savvy peer | 1-2 hours |
| **Annually** | Review your AI tools and strategy, attend the Aotearoa AI Summit | Half day |

This adds up to about 2-3 hours per month. That's a small investment for staying ahead of a technology that's reshaping how business works.

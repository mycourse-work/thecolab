# Your AI Action Plan

You've made it to the final lesson. Over the past eight modules, you've gone from "What is AI?" to understanding the tools, the law, the governance, and the future. That's a serious achievement — and you should feel good about it.

But knowledge without action is just trivia. So let's turn everything you've learned into a concrete plan you can start on Monday morning.

## Your 90-Day AI Action Plan

We're breaking this into three manageable phases. You don't need to do everything — pick what's relevant to your business and adjust the timeline to suit your pace.

### Phase 1: First 30 Days — Start Small, Learn Fast

The goal for month one is simple: **pick one AI tool, use it for one task, and track the results.**

- [ ] Choose one AI assistant to try (ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — all have free tiers)
- [ ] Pick one specific, repeatable task to use it for (email drafting, meeting summaries, social media posts, research)
- [ ] Spend 15-30 minutes per day using the tool for that task
- [ ] Track how much time you save compared to doing it manually
- [ ] Share what you're learning with at least one team member
- [ ] Sign up for the AI Forum NZ newsletter

> **The 30-day test:** If after 30 days you're saving even 30 minutes a week, that's 26 hours a year. At $50/hour, that's $1,300 in value from a free tool. Most people see results much bigger than this.

### Phase 2: Days 31-60 — Build the Foundation

Now that you've got a feel for how AI works, it's time to put some structure around it.

- [ ] Draft your AI workplace policy (use the template from Module 5 as a starting point)
- [ ] Run a team session — show your colleagues what you've been doing with AI, let them try it
- [ ] Identify your top 3 AI use cases based on the Impact/Effort Matrix from Module 4
- [ ] Check your Privacy Act obligations for any customer data you might use with AI tools
- [ ] Set up a simple AI tool inventory — which tools are being used, by whom, for what
- [ ] Explore whether the AI Advisory Pilot Programme or other NZ funding applies to your business

### Phase 3: Days 61-90 — Expand and Optimise

With a policy in place and your team on board, it's time to expand your AI toolkit and start measuring real business impact.

- [ ] Expand to 2-3 AI use cases across different parts of your business
- [ ] Implement basic AI governance using the AI Forum's AI Ready pathway
- [ ] Measure your ROI — compare time and cost savings against your AI tool costs
- [ ] Explore NZ-specific AI tools relevant to your industry (Xero JAX, Tradify, Cin7, etc.)
- [ ] Have a conversation with an AI consultant or attend a local AI event
- [ ] Plan your next 90-day cycle — what worked, what didn't, what's next

## What Separates the Businesses That Succeed

After studying hundreds of AI implementations, the pattern is clear. The businesses that get real value from AI share five traits:

1. **They start small.** One tool, one task, one person. Not a company-wide transformation on day one.
2. **They measure results.** Time saved, money saved, quality improved. They know their numbers.
3. **They involve their team.** AI adoption is a people challenge, not a technology challenge.
4. **They stay within the law.** Privacy Act, Fair Trading Act, workplace policies — they do it properly.
5. **They keep going.** The first attempt isn't always perfect. They iterate, adjust, and improve.

> None of these require a massive budget, a technical background, or a dedicated AI team. They require curiosity, a willingness to try, and the commitment to keep at it.

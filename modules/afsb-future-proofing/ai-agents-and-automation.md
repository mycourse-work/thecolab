# AI Agents and Autonomous Workflows

Up until now, we've been talking about AI as a tool you use — you type a question, it gives you an answer. You upload a document, it summarises it. That's genuinely useful, but it's only the beginning.

The next wave of AI is **agentic** — AI that doesn't just answer questions but actually *does things*. Books appointments. Processes invoices. Monitors your inventory and reorders stock before you run out. Think of it less like a search engine and more like a capable junior employee who never sleeps.

You don't need this today. But understanding where things are heading will help you make smarter choices about the tools you invest in right now.

## What Are AI Agents?

An AI agent is software that can take a goal, break it down into steps, and execute those steps independently — making decisions along the way. Instead of you telling it exactly what to do at every stage, you give it an objective and it figures out how to get there.

Here's a simple comparison:

| Traditional AI | AI Agent |
|---|---|
| You ask a question, it answers | You set a goal, it completes the task |
| One interaction at a time | Multiple steps, multiple tools |
| You drive the process | It drives the process (with guardrails) |
| "Draft me an email" | "Handle all overdue invoice follow-ups" |

> **Real-world example:** Xero's JAX is an early example of agentic AI in NZ business tools. Rather than just showing you your numbers, JAX can reconcile transactions, categorise expenses, run payroll, and generate forecasts — handling multi-step financial workflows that used to require you clicking through several screens.

## The Numbers Behind the Shift

This isn't hype — it's a market that's moving fast:

- The AI agent market is projected to grow from **$7.8 billion to $52 billion by 2030**
- **40% of enterprise applications** will embed AI agents by the end of 2026 (Gartner)
- **80% of enterprise workplace apps** will include AI copilots by 2026 (IDC)
- Inquiries about multi-agent systems surged **1,445%** in the past year

For context, when a technology grows that fast, it means the building blocks are already in place and businesses are actively demanding it.

## Protocols: Making AI Tools Talk to Each Other

One of the biggest developments happening right now is **protocol standardisation** — essentially, creating a common language so different AI tools can work together seamlessly.

Two protocols to be aware of:

- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — developed by Anthropic (the makers of Claude). Think of it as a universal plug that lets AI connect to your business tools, databases, and files.
- **A2A (Agent-to-Agent)** — developed by Google. This lets AI agents from different companies communicate and collaborate with each other.

Why does this matter for you? Because it means the AI tools you use won't be siloed. In the near future, your accounting AI will be able to talk to your inventory AI, which talks to your scheduling AI — without you acting as the go-between.

## What This Means for Your Small Business

Let's be practical about timelines:

**Now (2025-2026):** AI copilots are being embedded into the tools you already use — Xero, Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Shopify. You're getting AI assistance within your existing workflow. This is the stage to lean into.

**Near future (2026-2028):** AI agents will handle complete workflows. Imagine telling your AI: "Process this week's invoices, chase up anything overdue, and update the cashflow forecast." It does the whole thing.

**Further out (2028+):** Multi-agent systems will manage complex business operations. Your inventory agent notices a product is selling fast, checks supplier availability, places an order, updates your books, and adjusts your marketing — all without you lifting a finger.

> **Don't panic.** You absolutely don't need agentic AI today. But when you're choosing tools right now, it's worth asking: "Is this tool building towards agentic capabilities?" If two tools are similar but one is investing in agents and the other isn't, the first one will serve you better long-term.

## Practical Examples for Small Business

Here's what agentic AI could look like for different types of NZ small businesses:

- **Retail:** An AI agent monitors your sales data, predicts demand, automatically reorders stock from suppliers, and adjusts your online pricing based on inventory levels
- **Trades:** An agent reviews incoming job requests, checks your team's availability, sends quotes based on your pricing rules, and books confirmed jobs into your schedule
- **Professional services:** An agent handles client onboarding — sends welcome emails, sets up project folders, schedules the kick-off meeting, and generates the initial project brief
- **Hospitality:** An agent manages bookings, responds to common customer queries, adjusts staffing suggestions based on predicted demand, and follows up with guests post-visit

These aren't science fiction — companies are building them right now. Within two to three years, off-the-shelf versions will be available at small business price points.

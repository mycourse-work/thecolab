# Building an AI-Capable Team

You can have the best AI tools in the world, but if your team doesn't know how to use them — or doesn't want to — you're wasting your money. The number one barrier to AI integration isn't cost or technology. It's **skills**.

Only 24% of NZ workers have received any AI training. That's a massive gap — and it's your biggest opportunity. The businesses that invest in building AI capability now will have a significant head start over those that wait.

## The Three Approaches to Building AI Capability

When it comes to getting AI expertise into your business, you've got three options. Each has trade-offs:

| Approach | Cost | Timeline | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Upskilling** your existing team | $1,200-3,000 per person | Weeks to months | Preserves institutional knowledge, 40% reduction in consulting dependence | Takes time, requires motivation |
| **Hiring** AI-skilled people | High (salary + recruitment) | 6-12 months to full productivity | Brings fresh expertise | Expensive, competitive market, they don't know your business |
| **Outsourcing** to consultants | Variable (ongoing) | Immediate | Instant expertise, no training needed | Ongoing dependency, knowledge leaves when they do |

For most NZ small businesses, the right answer isn't one of these — it's a **hybrid approach**.

## The Hybrid Approach: What Actually Works

Here's the strategy that delivers the best results for small teams:

1. **Get 1-2 people trained well** — Send your most enthusiastic team members on a proper AI training course. These become your internal champions.
2. **Use a consultant for the initial setup** — Bring in outside expertise to help you choose tools, configure them, and build your first workflows.
3. **Have your champions train everyone else** — Once your early adopters are confident, they teach the rest of the team through hands-on sessions.

This gives you instant expertise (from the consultant), deep internal knowledge (from training), and sustainable capability (from peer teaching) — without breaking the bank.

> **The "Community of Multipliers" concept:** Identify your early adopters — the people who are already experimenting with ChatGPT or dabbling in AI on their own. Enable them, equip them, and let them become your internal AI champions. Peer teaching is more effective than top-down training because people learn better from colleagues they trust.

## Essential Skills at Each Level

**Stage 1: Prompt Engineering (everyone should learn this)**
- Writing clear, specific instructions for AI
- Providing context and examples for better results
- Knowing when AI output needs human review
- Understanding what AI can and can't do reliably

**Stage 2: AI Tool Fluency (key team members)**
- Confidently using your chosen AI tools daily
- Knowing which tool to use for which task
- Troubleshooting when AI gives poor results
- Integrating AI outputs into existing workflows

**Stage 3: Deploying AI Agents (your AI champion)**
- Setting up automated workflows using tools like Make.com
- Connecting AI to your business systems
- Monitoring agent performance and making adjustments
- Understanding data flow and security implications

**Stage 4: Advanced Capabilities (optional, for tech-forward businesses)**
- Evaluating and deploying open source AI models
- Building custom solutions for unique business needs
- Contributing to AI strategy and governance

## NZ Training Options

You don't need to figure this out alone. Here are real options available in New Zealand:

- **AI Innovisory** — Has trained 400+ NZ businesses since 2023. Practical, business-focused training.
- **Spark AI Foundations** — AI literacy programme from one of NZ's biggest tech companies.
- **AI Forum e-Learning** — Free modules covering AI fundamentals and ethics. Available at [aiforum.org.nz](https://aiforum.org.nz).
- **Regional workshops** — MBIE-funded AI workshops through chambers of commerce (like Tauranga's programme). Check with your local chamber.
- **The Co Lab** — Offers structured AI training programmes designed specifically for NZ small businesses. A good option if you want guided, practical learning for your team. Available at [thecolab.ai](https://thecolab.ai).
- **AI Advisory Pilot Programme** — Government-funded programme offering up to $15,000 in co-funding for AI roadmaps and implementation. Available through the Regional Business Partner Network.

> **Budget tip:** Start with the free AI Forum e-learning modules to build baseline knowledge across your team, then invest in deeper training for your 1-2 AI champions.

## Creating a Learning Culture

Training is just the beginning. The real magic happens when AI learning becomes part of your team's everyday culture. Here are practical ways to make that happen:

**Regular show-and-tell sessions**
Set aside 15 minutes in your team meeting for someone to share an AI win. "I used Claude to draft our client update email and it saved me 20 minutes." These small stories are more powerful than any training course.

**Share AI wins publicly**
Create a Slack channel, a shared document, or even a whiteboard where people post their AI discoveries. "Did you know ChatGPT can rewrite our FAQ in a friendlier tone?" Visibility breeds adoption.

**Allow experimentation time**
Give your team permission to try new things without pressure. "Spend 30 minutes this week experimenting with an AI tool for your work." Not every experiment will succeed, and that's fine.

**Celebrate the learners**
Recognise people who try new AI approaches — even if the results aren't perfect. The goal is to build a team that's curious and willing to experiment, not one that's afraid of getting it wrong.

**Make it safe to fail**
The biggest killer of AI adoption is fear — fear of looking silly, fear of making mistakes, fear of being replaced. Address this head-on. AI is a tool that makes people more effective, not a replacement for people.

## Key Takeaways

- The skills gap is the number one barrier to AI integration — only 24% of NZ workers have received AI training
- A hybrid approach works best: train 1-2 champions, use a consultant for setup, then have champions train everyone else
- AI skills build progressively: Prompt Engineering, Tool Fluency, Deploying Agents, Advanced Capabilities — most businesses only need the first two
- NZ has solid training options including AI Innovisory (400+ businesses trained), free AI Forum e-learning, and government co-funding up to $15,000
- Building a learning culture matters more than any single training course — regular show-and-tells, shared wins, and permission to experiment drive adoption

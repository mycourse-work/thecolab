# NZ Support and Funding

One of the best-kept secrets in New Zealand business is the amount of support available for AI adoption. Government co-funding, free frameworks, subsidised training, expert consultants — it's all there if you know where to look.

This lesson is your directory. Bookmark it and come back whenever you need it.

> **What you'll learn:** Navigate the AI Advisory Pilot Programme and determine your eligibility for up to $15,000 in co-funding | Identify additional NZ government funding options (R&D Tax Incentive, New to R&D Grant) | Access free resources from AI Forum NZ including governance frameworks and e-learning modules | Connect with NZ-based AI consultants and training providers suited to small business needs

## Government Funding

The NZ government has committed significant resources to helping businesses adopt AI. Here are the key programmes available right now.

### AI Advisory Pilot Programme

This is the big one for small businesses. The government is co-funding expert AI advice for SMEs through the Regional Business Partner Network.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **What** | Co-funded AI advisory services for small businesses |
| **How much** | Up to $15,000 per business (50% co-funding) |
| **Breakdown** | $2,500 for an AI roadmap + $12,500 for implementation support |
| **Who's eligible** | NZ small businesses |
| **How to apply** | Through your Regional Business Partner Network |
| **When** | January - June 2026 |
| **Website** | beehive.govt.nz |

This programme covers two stages. First, you get a $2,500 co-funded session with an AI expert who assesses your business and creates a tailored AI roadmap. Then, if you want to move forward, you can access up to $12,500 in co-funded implementation support — help with tool selection, setup, training, and integration.

> **Tip:** The programme is designed for 51+ businesses nationwide, so places are limited. If you're interested, apply early through your local Regional Business Partner.

### R&D Tax Incentive

If your AI project involves genuine research and development — testing new approaches, building custom solutions, or experimenting with novel applications — you may qualify for the R&D Tax Incentive.

- Administered by MBIE
- Available for businesses undertaking qualifying R&D, including AI projects
- Can reduce your tax liability on eligible R&D spending
- Website: mbie.govt.nz (search "R&D Tax Incentive")

This is more relevant if you're building something custom rather than using off-the-shelf tools, but it's worth checking if your AI work involves experimentation or development.

### New to R&D Grant

For businesses new to formal research and development:

- **How much:** 40% of eligible R&D costs, up to $400,000
- **Who:** Businesses new to R&D, including those applying AI in novel ways
- **How to apply:** Via funds.business.govt.nz
- **Note:** Callaghan Innovation grants are transitioning to MBIE, so check the current application process

### Other Government Support

- **Budget 2025** allocated $213 million for tuition and training subsidies, and $64 million for STEM education
- **Business.govt.nz** — the government's small business portal with general guidance on technology adoption, business planning, and compliance
- **NZ Institute for Advanced Technology** — $231 million investment (2025-2029) based in Auckland, focused on building NZ's AI capability

## AI Forum NZ

The [AI Forum](https://aiforum.org.nz) is New Zealand's leading AI industry body, and they offer an impressive range of free resources for businesses of all sizes.

### AI Governance Framework (Free)

A structured pathway to help your business govern AI responsibly. Three levels:

- **AI Ready** (entry-level) — 8 core practices including governance ownership, responsible AI principles, AI policy, risk assessments, and capability building. This is where most small businesses should start.
- **AI Smart** (intermediate) — 6 additional practices covering risk-based governance, vendor management, documentation, and performance monitoring.
- **AI Trusted** (advanced) — 6 practices for businesses wanting to meet international standards like ISO 42001.

Website: aigovernance.aiforum.org.nz

### AI e-Learning Modules (Free)

Two self-paced online courses:

1. **Introduction to AI** — Current trends and future impacts (aiforum.org.nz/lms/current-trends-and-future-impacts/)
2. **Implementing AI — Risks and Ethics** — Practical guidance on responsible implementation (aiforum.org.nz/lms/ethics-and-risks/)

### Other AI Forum Resources

- **AI Capability Directory** — Interactive map of the NZ AI ecosystem. Find providers, consultants, and tools
- **AI Productivity Reports** — Regular snapshots of how NZ businesses are using AI and what results they're getting
- **Networking events** — Including the annual **Aotearoa AI Summit**, which brings together businesses, government, and the AI community
- **AI Blueprint for Aotearoa** — Strategic vision for NZ's AI future

### Informal Learning

Don't underestimate the value of:

- **YouTube and online tutorials** — Free, practical, and constantly updated
- **AI tool documentation** — ChatGPT, Claude, and Copilot all have excellent help resources
- **Peer learning** — Talk to other business owners who are using AI. Most are happy to share what's working
- **Just experimenting** — Set aside 30 minutes a week to try something new with an AI tool
